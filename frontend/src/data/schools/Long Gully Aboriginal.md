23/4/1927 -- Public School Site at Long Gully. "IN August 1925, the
Lands Department intimated to this Department, that Mr. K. McKerrow of
Duri wished to purchase the Public School site at Long Gully being
Portion 29 of 2 acres and that Mr. McKerrow had been informed that
application should be made to this Department in the matter. As no
communication has been received in this Office from Mr. McKerrow, I
should be glad if you would kindly ascertain if possible, whether that
gentleman still desires to acquire the School site in question. Could
you in any way ascertain whether the site is fenced and whether any use
is being made of it?"

2/1/1933 -- Designation of Aboriginal School Long Gully -- Kindly note
that this Department has decided to adopt the designation "NGOORUMBA" in
lieu of "LONG GULLY" for the school above referred to."

Long Gully Parish -- Darley, County Hardinge -- Proposed designation
"Bethemek" or "Ngoorumba". Adopt "NGOORUMA" in lieu of "LONG GULLY".

4/10/1933 -- Long Gully Aborigines' School -- Appointment of a Teacher
-- "The Under Secretary, For some time, a Subsidised School for
Aboriginal children has been conducted at Long Gully, via Tingha. The
number of pupils has grown until, at the present time, there are between
20 and 30 enrolled. In response to a recent application by the
Australian Inland Missionary at Tingha, and with the concurrence of the
Board for the Protection of Aborigines', approval was given to the
establishment of an Aborigines' Provisional School to replace the
Subsidised School. The question of the appointment of a teacher has now
to be decided. In submitting his request for a Departmental school, the
Missionary, urged the appointment of his daughter, Miss. D. V. Harris.
She has successfully conducted the Subsidised School, and there is every
reason to believe that she has exerted a very favourable influence over
the children. Miss Harris is not a trained teacher, but holds the
Intermediate Certificate, obtained while a pupil of the St. George
Girls' High School. It appears to me that her qualifications would
compare very favourably with those generally held by persons appointed
as teachers of Aborigines' Schools. Miss Harris' appointment would be
acceptable to the Board for the Protection of Aborigines. I RECOMMEND,
therefore, that Miss. D. V. Harris be appointed in terms of Section 44
of the Public Service Act, 1902, as teacher of the Aborigines'
Provisional School at Long Gully, with pay at the rate of £3/14/7 per
week, subject to the provisions of the Public Service Salaries Act, 1931
-- 33. I further recommend that her appointment date from 12^th^
September, 1933 -- the date on which schools resumed after the Spring
Vacation." Approved

27/11/1933 -- Public School Long Gully -- "Further enquiries have been
made into your suggestion that the name "Sutherland's Water" be adopted
for this recently established Aboriginal School. The local Inspector of
Schools has been in touch with some of the residents in the locality and
it has been suggested that an aboriginal name such as "Ngoorumba",
meaning "a native home", or "Bethmek", meaning "house in the valley",
would be more appropriate. I shall be glad to have your views on this
matter."

13/11/1933 -- Letter to Dept. Education -- "Dear Sir, I reply to yours
of 6^th^ instant. The best suggestion I can give for a new name for the
Aboriginal School at Long Gully is Ngoorumba meaning a native home. The
pronunciation is as with a silent g and the u partly like oo. It is a
named which was used years ago at a native school at Stony Creek not
many miles away from Long Gully and is remembered by some of the parents
of the present scholars. If this is not suitable I would offer as an
alternative Bethemek, meaning, house in the valley. Thank you for the
opportunity given."

2/1/1934 -- Letter to Australian Inland Mission -- "Dear Sir, With
reference to your communication of the 13^th^ November, addressed to Mr.
L. F. Keller, Inspector of Schools, regarding a suggestion that a new
name be found for the Aboriginal School at Long Gully, I desire to
inform you that your suggestion that the school be named "Ngoorumba"
Aboriginal School has been adopted. Kindly inform all interested
parties."
