**Role of the Department and relationship to the APB**

11 October 1909 -- Certificate of resumption of land resumed at School
at Mundoonan

3 October 1911 -- Memo from Dept. Public Instruction re Site and
erection of building Mundoonan -- "The Aboriginal settlement at
Mundoonan is now completely established. There are 23 children at
present enrolled and there will be about 6 additional in a short time.
At present the children occupy part of an old kitchen which is most
uncomfortable and unsuitable. It will be necessary to have a 2 acre site
allotted in the reserve in the possession of the AP Board. There is a
very suitable site adjacent to the residence. I recommend that the AP
Board be approached with a view to having a site dedicated. Under
separate cover I forward plans and specification." Note from Chief
Inspector "I was informed by the Manger that the enrolment would be 30,
but up to the March quarter of this year it has not exceeded 22 and
average has not been above 18. The experience of these schools is that
enrollment fluctuates considerably and attendance is very intermittent.
I am of the opinion that the building proposed will meet all
requirements here. I think that the Board might fairly be asked to pay
half the cost."

11 Dec 1911 -- APB to Dept. Education -- Provision of a substitute
teacher during absence of Mr. H. E. Hockey. "Sir, I have the honor, by
direction of the Board for the Protection of Aborigines, to inform you
that Mr. H. E. Hockey, Manager and Teacher at the Aboriginal Station,
Edgerton (Yass), has been granted three weeks leave of absence from his
duties as Manager, to be taken during the forthcoming School holidays.
It will be necessary to send a substitute, and it is understood that Mr.
Balfour Morgan, Teacher in your Department, is willing to perform the
duties for a similar allowance to that paid to Mr. Hockey. I am to ask
if your Department will kindly sanction this arrangement."

1911 -- Description of Land -- Two acres in the County of Murray. Supply
of water -- By means of an iron tank. There is no timber on the site,
the ground has been used for cultivation. Crown Land or private
property? -- "It is Crown Land, vested in the A.P. Board.

4 March 1913 -- Copy of letter sent to APB from Dept. Public Instruction
"Sir, with reference to your letter, dated the 5^th^ Ultimo, respecting
the erection of a new school building for Aborigines at Mundoonan, I
have to inform you that in view of the Auditor Generals ruling in the
case of Pilliga School, the Minister of Public Instruction is not
prepared to accede to the request for payment of half the cost of the
building from the votes of this Department."

APB -- 13 March 1913 -- Erection of school building for Aborigines at
Mundoonan (Edgerton). "On the 31^st^ January the Board approved of the
half cost (£55) being borne by this Department. The Department of Public
Instruction were so informed and the Under Secretary now informs the
Board that, in view of the recent ruling of the Auditor General, the
Minister of Public Instruction is not prepared to approve of he
half-cost being charged to the vote of his Department. The matter is now
submitted for the Board's authority to charge the whole cost to the
Department. -- The Board for the Protection of Aborigines will be
prepared to pay the whole cost of this building, but there are no funds
available at the present time for the purpose." Signed Chairman APB.

14 April 1913 -- Parliament House "The Under Secretary Department of
Public Instruction SYDNEY -- Dear Sir, Some few days ago I wrote you re
the payment to Messrs. Webster & Newman for the erection of a school at
Mundoonan, in the district of Yass. Your department owes these men
£125.17.6, also the sum of £6.6.0 deposit. Now, as I pointed out the
other day to you, these men owe a portion of this money, and as it is
long since the work has been completed, it seems very strange and unfair
to me that men earning their living by this work should be kept out of
their earnings. The treatment is unjust and if it were a private
contract it would be paid the morning of completion. It savours very
much of red-tapism that money should be kept back for work done for
upwards of two months, and certainly is not business and is one of the
incidents which brings departmental administration into odium. I got a
formal reply from you some days ago on this matter which as far as I can
see seems to have closed the thing. This is the last time I shall write
but shall advise these men to institute proceedings without further
delay for the recovery , adding interest on the money owing, as well.
Hoping you will fix this up without further trouble." Note from Dept.
Pub Instruction "Papers re payment of £125.17.6 to AP Board as it is an
Aboriginal School, on 27.3.1913."

Letter from contractor 15 April 1913 Newman to Dept. Public Instruction
asking for money -- note on bottom that papers sent to AP Board on
27.3.1913.

16 April 1913 -- Police Department, Inspector General's Office Sydney
Subject: Erection of School Building at Mundoonan (Edgerton Aboriginal
Station). "The building in question was erected by the Department of
Public Instruction, and, as already stated, the Aborigines Protection
Board are prepared to pay a sum of £ 55 towards the cost of erection.
The sum of £500 voted on the Board's estimates for "Cost of erection of
school buildings and Teachers residences" is already exhausted, and the
£55 will come out of the Board's general Vote, and will be paid if a
certified claim is rendered to this Department." Secretary A.P Board.
Note from Under Sectary of Public Instruction "Submitted. This is a
matter that cannot stand over until July the school having been erected
and completion report and certified claim having been received ... it is
submitted that under these circumstances, and in view of the state of
the AP Boards Vote, this Dept. should pay the balance of £70-17-6"
