Transfer of teacher -- filed under urunga.

24/6/1913 -- Aboriginal Station Sevington -- Chief Inspector of Schools
-- Purchase of fasteners

22/5/1913 -- School has not opened yet -- letter asking for the purchase
of sundries. "The building has been erected by the Aborigines Protection
Board -- The Department of Public Instruction has been asked by them to
provide school furniture and utensils. I write to state that the station
is situated 20 miles from Wellingrove ... The head quarters of the
station which is controlled by a Local Aborigines Committee under APB
Sydney is situation in Inverell, which is 30 miles distant, and Glen
Innes 35 miles."

18/2/1913 -- Correspondence concerning the purchase of fasteners for
windows and furniture.

Further correspondence concerning purchase of fasteners -- 9/4913 --
District Inspector of Schools writes -- "When I state that Sevington
School is not in my district, perhaps I should state that I have not
heard of the place. Possibly it is a recently opened school, in my
district, official n oticeof which has not yet been sent to me."

Request to APB 12/3/1913 concerning the purchase of desks, chairs, and
other tools necessary for the school. Member of Local APB Committee
writes "Forwarded and Recommended. The Local Committee has not asked for
quotations for these supplies in Inverell, as it is probable the Board
either has them available, or could procure more cheaply in Sydney."
Signed Rupert Fairbrother.

APB -- 25/2/1913 -- Furniture and Material for Aborigines School at
Sevington via Wellingrove. "Forwarded, by direction of the Board for
Protection of Aborigines, for favor of consideration by the Department
of Public Instruction. A suitable school building, 26' \* 20' \* 12',
with an 8' verandah in front and brick chimney at the side, has recently
been erected by the Board, and the Manager is at present imparting
instruction to the aboriginal children. Perhaps, under the
circumstances, the attached request for furniture and material may be
complied with." Secretary AP Board to Under Secretary of Public
Instruction.

29/9/1914 -- Sevington re: Date of Conversion from subsidized to
Provisional. "Miss Hagan took charge of the Sevington Subsidised school
on 18^th^ May last. The attendance from date of opening was well over
10; and now is about 20. I find that, I forwarded recommendation for
conversion of the school from subsidized to Provisional on 8.8.1914. I
recommend that -- if the date has not already been fixed -- the
conversion of the school by Provisional, dates from 1^st^ September.
Miss Hagan to be paid salary accordingly, and to be informed."

19/8/1914 -- Dept. Public Instruction -- Proposal to establish a
Provisional School in place of existing subsidized school. -- goes on to
write -- "... A good building has been erected by the Aborigines
Protection Board, and furnished by the Department. The station is well
managed and there is good reason to believe that the present enrolment
will soon show an increase." Submitted and approved.

Arrangements made by Department of Public Instructed after the
conversion of the school for "an additional supply of school material to
be forwarded."

17/4/1914 - Memo from Manager Aboriginal Station Sevington -- to APB per
Inverell Local Committee -- "The Manager begs to report that 3 of the 4
children of school age expected on 2^nd^ instant duly arrived -- the
fourth a girl of 12 is in service in Ashford. There are now 15 children
of School age on the station. To date no other families have arrived,
but shortly after the opening of the school, it is almost certain 2 or 3
more families will come. There are at present 71 Aborigines here, the
greatest number, except for a few days at ...., that has ever been on
the station. A report will be furnished, as directed, when any
additional families arrive on the station."

6/4/1914 -- APB -- Sydney -- Subject: School for Aborigines at Sevington
Reserve -- "The matter of the appointment of a Teacher at Sevington
Station has already been brought under the notice of the Department of
Public Instruction. Please report when the additional families referred
to, have taken up their residence and state the number of children of
school age on the station." -- Note from Local Committee -- "Will the
Manager kindly report through the Local Committee if the four children
expected on April 2^nd^ have come to the Station, and if there is any
further sign of the two or three families taking up their residence?

3/4/1914 -- Memo to the Secretary APB Sydney per Local Committee
Inverell from Manager Aboriginal Station Sevington -- The manager begs
report that 12 Aborigines arrived from Bushfield on 28^th^ instant to
take up the permanent residence on the Station. There are now 12
children of school age on the station, four more are due April 2^nd^.
There is a proposed 2 or 3 families coming in the near future. There is
a considerable amount of petty jealousy existing between the two
factions, and which careful handling is necessary to make them coalesce.
The Aborigines from the Bushfield are particularly anxious to get their
children to school. The manager has deemed it (?) to provide that it
will be positively opened after the easter vacation by Aprl 20. The
school is the only thing that will hold those from Bushfield if it is
not as promised on April 20 they will certainly leave, and I think not
come back again to Sevington- will the Board having my promises and have
the school opened in terms of my application of Feb 10 last. Formal
application is also made now for the granting of the material (books and
stationary) necessary in opening new schools." Note from Local Committee
-- The Local Committee has already strongly recommended the appointment
of a teacher, and the Manager has offered to arrange to board the
teacher. The opening of the school will be a stronger lever to attract
and hold some families that are wandering about, and the Committee
trusts that the Board will be able to authorize a 'subsidised' or other
teacher without delay.

5/4/1914 -- APB note concerning whether the teacher has taken up their
residence at Sevington. Note from Chief Inspector -- "The Manager begs
to report that Miss Hagan opened the subsidized school on the station on
18 May,; and has an enrolment of 10 girls 15 boys total 25 all of school
age, with an average daily attendance of 90%. The opening of the school
has had the derived effect in drawing to the station all the aborigines
practically scattered throughout the district. There being 91 at present
on the station. There is as far as can he see now as likelihood of
anything arising that will make the numbers less than a fair average
Provisional school. Application is now made that steps be taken to have
the school opened as a Provisional School and the supply of material
supplied to the teacher, being a for a subsidized school with a minimum
of 10 pupils, is altogether inadequate for 25, the manager strongly
recommends that a Provisional School's equipment be forwarded to the
teacher advised via Glen Innes.

**Teachers**

APB -- 18/9/1911 -- Proposed employment of Miss. E. P. Hagan as Teacher
at the School for Aborigines Erambie Cowra (School Estray) The Board
considers this a very suitable appointment. Note from Department. "I
recommend that Miss. E. P. Hagan be temporarily employed as Teacher of
the Aborigines' School at Erambie, with salary at the rate of £100 per
annum from the date of her entry on duty. Miss Hagan should eb given to
understand that this appointment gives her no claim to any other
position in the Service." Approved.

Letter from Miss. Hagan to Chief Inspector Dept Public Instruction -- "
Application for appointment to change of Aborigines School" - "I beg to
offer myself as a candidate for temporary employment as teacher of an
Aborigines Provisional School. I have been associated with Aborigines'
Mission work for a period of nine months and am therefore conversant
with the lives and conditions of the people. I was educated at the
Darling Road Superior Public School Rozelle. My age is twenty seven
years. Enclosed is one testimonial of my character. If appointed I am
prepared to go to the Clarence or Cowra districts and will strictly
conform to all Departmental regulations as well as do my best to promote
the best interests of the pupils and adults also."

Letter from Missionary training home, Melbourne -- to Department of
Education concerning Miss. Hagan. "Miss Hagan, I believe, is applying to
the Australian Aborigines Mission -- I thought you would like to know
particulars concerning her. Miss Hagan has been a student in "St Hildas"
for about eighteen months. She entered as a candidate for the 'China
Inland Mission'. Miss Hagan is a good, earnest girl, but we feel she has
not the intellectual ability to study of the Chinese Language. It has
therefore been decided, that she will not go to China. I believe she is
fitted to work among the Aborigines. She is most unselfish, always ready
and willing to render any service in her power. I should be glad to know
she was engaged in definite work for God."

6/10/1914 -- Letter from Eva. Hagan to Department of Public Instruction
asking for permission to sit for the next teachers examination in Easter
1915, for the purposes of trying for a 3B classification. Reply from
Department -- "Miss Hagan ifnromed that she has not been admitted to the
Teaching service, she cannot be allowed to attend exam for
Classification..."

Miss. Eva. P. Hagan -- Teacher in Charge Sevington -- 17 December 1923
-- Memo to Dept Education concerning entitlement to an advance in salary
after having completed 12 years service. "On December 1^st^ 1923, I
completed 12 years service under Section 44. Being unclassified, I would
like to know if I am entitled to a rise in my salary." Note from
Department -- Miss Hagan is eligible for an increase in her salary.
Increase given at £19/10 per annum.

2/11/1916 -- Teacher Hagan -- Memo to Department concerning application
for permission to sit for 3B Classification next June. "I have the honor
to apply for permission to sit for class III Examination for next June.
I have not been through Hereford House, as I took up teaching before
that came into force. I have been teaching almost five years." Signed
Hagan. Note from Department in Reply -- "Inform that as she as not been
admitted to the teaching service she cannot be allowed to attend
examination for classification. In order to gain admission to the
service it will be necessary for her to pass the qualifying entrance
examination for Small School Teachers and undergo a course of six months
training."

23/11/1914 -- Letter to Inspector of Schools Inverell from Teacher Eva.
P. Hagan re: Application for permission to teach on Saturday December
12^th^ in lieu of Friday December 18^th^ 1914. "Sir, I beg to apply for
permission to teach on Saturday December 12^th^ in lieu of Friday
December 18^th^ 1914. My reason for doing so, is so as to enable me to
leave for my home which is in Drummoyne. Otherwise if I do not leave on
Friday, I cannot leave till Monday which means three or four days of the
holidays gone, before you reach home. Another reason is that the School,
which is situated 35 miles from the Railway Station, which means an
eight or nine hours drive and is to be considered in this weather."
