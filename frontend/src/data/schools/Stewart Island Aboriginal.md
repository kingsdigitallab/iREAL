**Role of the Department and relationship to the APB**

10 June 1916 -- APB to Dept. Ed. Re Asking for school material "Sir, I
beg to inform you that I am in receipt of a report from the Local Police
to the effect that the Aborigines School building now in course of
erection at Stewart's Island Reserve, Nambucca Heads, is nearing
completion, and to ask that arrangements be made to forward necessary
School material, furniture etc. The question of the nomination of a
Teacher is now receiving consideration."

19 June 1916 -- Memo to Mr. Beauman Sussex Street, Sydney re Stewart
Island Reserve (Aboriginal School) "Further inquiry has been made of the
Secretary A. P. Board, in regard to this school. He suggests that the
dispatch of material etc. be deferred for the present. He is making
inquiries as to the route to the school and as to the taking delivery of
the supplies on arrival, and will communicate with the Under Secretary
when he has obtained the information."

15 August 1916 - Letter from APB to Under Secretary Dept. Ed "I beg to
inform you that I am in receipt of a report from the Officer in charge
of Police at Nambucca Heads, to the effect that furniture and material
for the Aborigines School at Stewart's Island, has not yet arrived.
Under the circumstances, perhaps its supply could be expedited, the
newly appointed teacher (Miss H.O. M Ferguson of C/O Mrs. B. H. Chapman,
"Broughlea", Pymble, being advised when it has been dispatched from
Sydney, so that she can arrange to leave at the earliest possible moment
thereafter, for the purposes of entering on duty."

24 October 1916 - Memo to Dept. Ed re additional furniture required.
"There is at present seating accommodation at this school for only 16
pupils. There are 18 pupils on the roll and 4 to come next week." Supply
of furniture approved.

7 July 1919 - Memo from Chief Inspector Dept. Ed -- "Stewart Island
Aboriginal School Attached papers regarding action of Miss Hagan,
Teacher. As long as Miss Hagan's activities do not affect the proper
discharge of her school duties, nor offend the religious convictions of
any section of the public, there is nothing in the regulations as far as
I can see to control her actions. Everything is in excellent order at
the Urunga Aboriginal Station, and Miss Hagan's sense of the fitness of
the (??) should tell her that it is an imperative on her part to attempt
to influence the (??)\> as the stations are for their general management
under the Board for the Protection of Aborigines, that body could (??)
if they deem it to be desirable, prevent the employee on the station
acting in an unsolicited capacity on another. I recommend that the
matter be left to the Aborigines Board." Note I think the remedy here
lies with the A. P Board, which should have the power of excluding from
a reserve persons likely to disturb the peace of the aborigines.

30 July 1919 -- Letter to APB from Dept. Ed "With reference to your
communication dated 26^th^ ultimo, No. 4348, relative to Miss Hagan
undertaking Missionary duties at Urunga Aborigines Station, I desire to
inform you that as far has her position under this Department is
concerned, Miss Hagan is not infringing any regulation of this
Department, provided her action does not interfere with the welfare of
her school. The matter is, therefore, one for the Aborigines Protection
Board to deal with.'

Aborigines Station Urunga -- "Memo sent to Miss Hagan in this matter 26-
12- 1919 Dear Miss Hagan, Yours of the 16^th^ Instant to hand re matter
of holding a Service etc on this Station this coming week end. In reply
I would like to state that both Mrs Smithers and self are generally
absent from the Station on holidays and week ends. Regular Services are
held on this Station by the Rev Symonds the C. E. Minister at Bellengen
who visits here on week days that are convenient. In any case I would
like to suggest that you should communicate with the Secretary of the
Board before making Missionary visits to other Stations. As in any case
the Secretary's approval is always necessary in anything of that sort."
Yours Faithfully Ernest. C. Smithers. Note underneath "Memo Dear Mr.
Foot I am sending you Miss Hagan's letter also my reply to the Letter,
for some time now that Nambucca Reserve has been a semi-Missionary camp
and judging by the exhibitions of testifying etc I have heard of we have
no wish to see it repeated here. Our People are quiet, we have Services
and conversations by ordained Minister's of Religion, and I would
suggest that the Missionary element be kept out of it. I feel sure that
fellow Long is at the back of Miss Hagan. I reckon Inspector Sheehy
would buck if he knew Long was instructing his Teacher. I think a nice
note from you would let Miss Hagan know what her real position is."

**Teachers**

> *Experience*

Letter of application for position of teacher of Aboriginal school. "To
the Chief Health Office Darwin.. Sir, In reference to the advertisement
in the Sydney Daily Telegraph of the 22^nd^ August 1917; for a woman
teacher in a Primary School of 50 pupils, I respectfully ask to be
considered an applicant for the position. I have been teaching in a
"provisional school" for a period of twelve months under the Education
Department of New South Wales. Prior to this, I was teaching in a
Mission School in New Guinea, so I am used to a tropical climate. I am
making this application through the Inspector W.G. Harvey Esq, H. M
Inspector of Schools for this District and should this application be
favorably considered I am prepared if necessary to furnish further
particulars and credentials. I am 35 years of age. Ii have the honor Sir
most respectfully." Hilda Ferguson

Letter from Dept. Ed to Ms Ferguson re application for appointment to
Darwin. Asking that Hilda direct her enquiries to the Federal Public
Service Inspector as the NSW dept. has not been approached in connection
with the provision of a teacher for Darwin.

10 June 1919 -- Memo from Teacher to Dept. Ed re Application for leave

under water". Leave asked for Tuesday May 28^th^, Thursday May 29^th^
and Friday May 30^th^.

June 1920 -- "Provisional of urinal. Extract from Medical Officer's
report of 18^th^ December last. "In the Sanitary arrangements for this
school it was found on inspection that there was no urinal for the
boys." Note from Chief Inspector -- "The enrolment of boys in the March
Quarter was 9i. This is an Aborigines' School. A urinal need not be
supplied." Seen no action necessary

2 December 1927 -- Memo re closure of school re wet weather. November
29^th^ and 30^th^ as approach to school being under water.

Chief Inspector Dept. Ed report on Building site etc -- Inspection June
8^th^, 1928 -- "CI, The building is on an island, connected with the
mainland by a causeway. The island is low lying. There is now only one
family living on the island. During the flood times and excessive high
tides the island is cut off from the mainland. Children and teacher are
then able to attend only by crossing by boat. At the time of inspection
most of the land surrounding the school was a swamp. The site then
appears to be an unhealthy one. The A.P.B should be approached so that
the expediency of transferring the school to the suitable position on
the mainland might be considered. The building is infested with white
ants and should be treated by an expert. It also requires lining and
painting inside. It is recommended that the necessary repairs be arrived
out after the visiting of the white ant expert. Perhaps its removal
might precede the necessary repairs. It is suggested that a cooking
stove, white deal table, and the like be installed so that some domestic
training may be given to the girls. A carpentry bench would also be
desirable."

**Students**

> *Conditions*

4 June 1936 -- Letter from Teacher to Dept. Ed. "I have to report that
Juanita Buchannan is away from school with Blight. She is almost better
now, I believe. She might be quite better." Signed Alice. M. Higgins

10 June 1936 -- Letter to Teacher from Dept. Ed re case of ophthalmia in
your school "The case of ophthalmia, reported by you, has been noted,
and I am forwarding herewith pamphlets on epidemic ophthalmia, for your
information and guidance."

24 March 1936 -- Letter from Teacher to Dept. Ed -- "Sir, some months
ago I kept one of the girls in after school. I forget what for and
cannot say whether she deserved the detention as nearly as I remember. I
only kept her a few minutes, but I asked her if she would lift one of
the seats onto the desk. She dropped it and hurt her leg. Afterwards she
was hit with a ball while playing rounders. There is still an open sore
on her left and near it her leg does not look right. Of course I did not
know it would be sore so long. Should an inquiry of this kind be
reported? As nearly as I remember she only missed school one day on
account of her leg. I gave her a bottle of peroxide today and I am
urging her to endeavour to heal it, but I wonder whether it would break
down again. She says she has treated it." Signed Miss Alice Higgins.

Letter from student Kathleen Davies age 13 April 9 1936 -- "I was kept
in after school because I went to town and did not get back till late.
After school was over Teacher told me to put the stool upon the desk it
fell on my shin and bruised it. It did not feel sore until the next day.
Miss Higgins gave me some lotion and it is getting better now."

15 Feb 1939 -- Memo from Teacher to Dept. Ed -- re two children with
sore eyes " Dear Sir, I have to inform you that two children are away
from school with sore eyes. I do not know whether the trouble is Blight"
Miss Alice Higgins. Note: Forwarded for consideration of the PMO

1^st^ March 1939 -- Letter to Teacher Aborigines School Stewart Island
re -- Outbreak of eye trouble re your memo of 15.2.1939 -- "I wish to
acknowledge receipt of your memo of 15.2.29, reporting an outbreak of
eye trouble amongst the children of the school. You are not certain, you
state, whether the condition is "sandy blight". Whether it is or not,
these inflammatory conditions of the eyes are usually infectious, and
when a teacher is in doubt the affected children should be excluded from
school until the inflammation has cleared up. Pamphlet on ophthalmia is
enclosed herewith, for your information."

**Other comments**

26 Feb 1929 -- Memo from Chief Inspector re Stewart Island Aborigines --
Children unable to attend school "CI, As the name implies the school is
situated on an island. This island is low lying and no dark people are
living on it. They have moved to the mainland. Communication with the
Island was maintained by means of causeway which the recent floods have
broken away for some considerable distance. The teacher is conveyed by a
leaky punt to her post, but the majority of the pupils has no means of
reaching the school. I suggest that the A.P Board be approached and
asked to rent temporarily a cottage on the mainland in order that school
work can be carried on till permanent arrangements can be made."

Letter sent to AP Board re above matter. With the added information that
for the December quarter of last year the total number of enrolment was
19.

13 August 1935 -- No attendance of pupils at school. "Nearly all the
children had colds or influenza."
