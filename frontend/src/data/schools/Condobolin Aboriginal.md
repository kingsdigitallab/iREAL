**Role of the Department and relationship to the APB**

13/1/1925 -- APB to Dept Education re Establishment of an Aborigines
School at Condobolin -- In accordance with a telephone request I now beg
on behalf of my Board to formally recommend the establishment of a
School for Aborigines on the Reserve at Condobolin. A school was
formerly conducted on the Reserve under the direction of the local
Missionary, Miss Wiezner, and this was inspected by Mr. Inspector Ewing
on the 23^rd^ of October last when he found that there was an enrolment
of fourteen and that owing to Miss Wiezner having to absent herself from
Condobolin in connection with Missionary work at other centres the
school was opened at irregular intervals and he therefore recommended
that it be made continuous in its operations. The Directors of the
Mission also have intimated to my Board their desire that Miss Wiezner
be relieved of her school duties, and under those circumstances it is
now therefore recommended that a school be established on a proper basis
and Mrs. L. R. Wilkins appointed as teacher in accordance with the
recommendation contained in my previous communication referred to
above."

2/2/1926 -- Letter to Dept Education re Condobolin Aboriginal School
report re establishment -- CI: As previously indicated by me there is a
need for a full time school on the Aborigines Settlement at Condobolin.
Mrs. L. R. Wilkins has taken up the duties there (21-1-26). The building
is not an ideal one but may be improved later on especially the
ventilation. A full supply of material is needed at the school.
Application for this has already been sent in." Annotation --
Establishment approved. Supply materials.

21/10/1930 -- APB to Dept. Education re Protest by Mrs. L. R. Wilkins
against removal from Condobolin to Pooncarie -- "In view of the medical
certificate furnished by Mrs. Wilkins to your Department in which it is
stated that a transfer from Condobolin to Pooncarie would be inimical to
her health, and also that it would be highly inadvisable for her not to
be within reach of a doctor, it is now recommended that Mrs. Wilkins be
given an appointment as Teacher of the Aborigines School at
Gulargambone, vice Mrs. Agnes Mercer, who states that she will be
compelled to resign on account of ill health, and will only carry on her
duty until relieved by her successor. Suitable accommodation can be
secured near the School by Mrs. Wilkins, and a doctor also resides in
the town. It is requested that urgent action be taken in this matter, as
Mr. L. N. Briggs, who has been nominated for the position of Teacher at
Condobolin, is at present residing in the town on leave from my Board,
and waiting instructions to take up duty. A separate communication will
be address to your Department regarding the filling of the vacancy at
Pooncarie." AC.Pettit

7/3/1934 -- APB to Dept. Education re Supply of form and Desk to
Condobolin "I beg to advise having received a request from the Teacher,
Aborigines School, Condobolin, that one form, and one school desk (each
to accommodate four) be supplied, in view of the fact that there is at
present an attendance of 27 pupils, and accommodation for only 24. Under
the circumstances perhaps your Department could arrange the supply of
these articles accordingly." Approved.

26/9/1936 -- Request for Fuel allowance from Teacher to Department of
Education

8/10/1936 -- Letter from Department of Education to Teacher Briggs re
Fuel Allowance "I desire to inform you that it is no the practice of
this Department to grant any allowance towards the purchase of fuel at
Aboriginal Schools. It is suggested that you approach the Aborigines
Protection Board in this connection."

15/7/1938 -- APB to Dept. Education re Condobolin Aborigines School
additions "I have to advise that the matter of the erection of additions
to the Aborigines' School at Condobolin received consideration at a
recent Meeting of my Board, when it was decided that in view of more
urgent works requiring attention on other reservations, the above would
have to stand over for the time being. It will, however, be kept under
notice for consideration at a later date."

> 23/6/1926 -- Memo from Teacher Wilkins to Dept. Education re illness
> "Dear Sir, for over a week I have been suffering from Influenza as
> mostly all of the children and parents have had a slight attack. I've
> tried to hold onto my duties, till end of term. However, a large
> abscess and information formed onto a piece of teeth left in the jaw,
> after an extraction and for 48 hours I suffered agonies. Capt Keyman
> (Sal. Army) came for the, scrip lesson in school yesterday and after
> the lesson, he kindly drove me around the Dr. who advised my going to
> the dentist. A slight operation was performed on the jaw bone,
> removing decayed bone, and abscess. The Dr. said I must remain in bed,
> and let treatment and rest, heal the wound and give the influenza a
> chance to dispense. He said I was just knocked up trying to hold up
> till the end of the term. As, its only 3 remaining days to end of
> term, and the not missed one day from personal illness all the term
> I'm sure you will feel I am justified in asking for the three days;
> now if I can travel I will go to Lithgow for a week, and be quite well
> to return to my duties after vacation. .....
>
> 31/6/1927 -- Claims for workers compensation (various correspondence)
> mytosis of the back.
>
> Includes various Dr. Certificates and letters from teacher explaining
> that illness is caused from bad conditions in the building.
>
> Doctor considered that it was caused through her having to work in a

> 11/7/1927 -- Letter from Teacher Wilkins to Inspector Laird "Dear Sir,
> Will you kindly forward the following account for the proper Dept. for
> me. I got better, during the holidays. Also, school children, all lost
> colds and aches etc. and as soon as we get 2 or 3 days in the school,
> colds are our lot again. Children and self are working very hard to
> receive a prize for some of our work, at the show, and we are working
> in not only discomfort but shivering with cold, most days. The unlined
> building is dreadful in winter and intense heat in summer. It is very
> wet night, the official paper at residence. Boys leave with mail at
> 8.45 am. "
>
> 2/7/1927 -- Dept Education re Mr. Inspector Lairds report. Condobolin
> Aboriginal School: Request for medical inspection. "It is difficult to
> see what immediate value medical inspection will have in this case.
> The home conditions regarding food, clothing, and shelter appear
> unsatisfactory. I suggest they be brought under the notice of the
> Aborigines Protection Board. The school building might well be
> improved, presuming the teacher's statement is correct. No visit has
> been paid in recent years to this school nor is any medical officer
> likely to be in the hear neighborhood. If desired by the Chief
> Inspector a special visit could be paid, but it means practically
> sending a medical officer from Sydney for this purpose only. If this
> is decided on I would suggest that the District Inspector's report
> should first be obtained; and indeed, he should be able to deal with
> the whole of the matters mentioned. Harvey Suttons Principal Medical
> Officer- Forward copy of teachers report to A.P Board for any action
> deemed necessary, and in particular ask if anything could be done to
> make conditions at the school more comfortable."
>
> 22/6/1927 -- Memo from Teacher Wilkins to Dept Education (Laird) re
> Unlined School building -- "Dear Sir, I had been hoping your visit of
> inspection would take place before these holidays commenced. As I
> wanted you to see our conditions here. The school building is unlined.
> Its built of iron and batterns just an unlined frame. Two doors and
> two windows, a large open fireplace. IN winter, with cold winds its
> bitter. Children all have bad colds; earache cry with pain in faces
> and heads in the middle of lesson, or cold feet and hands. They say:
> Matron, The cold is coming in my ear from the holes. I move about I
> let them sit on their little bare feet and warm their hands (Note to
> Wendy -- not sure if this page follows directly it appears to be a
> continuation of the memo) ... 2 weeks. Wood very expensive here, about
> 10 small logs for £10 A.P Board gave me an oil stove. I use this and
> warm the room .Results are in May Children' s work was 9/10, In June
> 3/10, average all through. July and August its nerve racking strain,
> to try to get the simplest lessons done. They go deaf: dull, and seem
> too cold too, too dull, half fed, badly clothed living in tents and
> falling down houses poor little things end it up, in a cold
> schoolroom. What chance has a teacher. 18 months ago, 4 Elder girls,
> now in service were all that could read, or write or anything and that
> only Primer I. Children would swear, run out of room, throw books, ink
> wells at me, or each other. That was my start, and a bit of an unlined
> room to live in myself. Dr. Percy suggested yesterday, I just wrote to
> you this letter, telling you this, you will see what I've aimed at:
> But at present I too have gone down. To the School conditions for two
> weeks, I've been ill. Struggling on, and not missed an hour. But at
> last I have in to Dr. Percy and rather then let the children loose
> time, I will do a long morning while school is warm, and then I'll
> have to loose about 3/4 of an hour. For the next 3 days. As I am
> suffering itense pain with "Muscular Rheumatism" ......

> 1/4/1931 -- Letter to Chief Inspector of Schools from Mr Briggs re
> Wire Fence for School Garden. "Dear Sir, I beg to inform you that
> there is no fence enclosing the school grounds on the Aborigines'
> Reserve at Condobolin. As the school building is situated near to the
> river bank it would be possible and very profitable to give my pupils
> suitable instruction in vegetable gardening if we had enough wire
> netting with which to enclose the school grounds. I would suggest that
> the Department of Education supply this school with 3 rolls (50 yards
> each) of wire netting 72 X 2 X19 and 300 yards of ordinary fence wire.
> There are sufficient posts on the Reserve with which to erect the
> fence. All the labour would be performed by fathers of the school
> children." Note from Chief Inspector "As this is an Aboriginal School,
> the matter might be brought under the attention of the Aborigines'
> Protection Board."
