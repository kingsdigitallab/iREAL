[**Role of the Department and relationship to the APB**]

16/3/1938 -- APB Subject: Formal establishment of Aboriginal School
Tabulum. "I beg to advise that, following upon representations made to
it some time ago, my Board approved of the erection of a number of
buildings upon the Aborigines Reserve at Tabulum, including a school.
The contract has now been practically completed and I therefore beg to
recommend the formal establishment of an Aborigines school at this
centre, and for the necessary desks, forms and other equipment and
material to be forwarded without delay. The size of the building is 12 8
20. The appointment of Mr. C. W. Brearley, the present Assistant Teacher
of the Aboriginal School at Woodenbong, as Teacher in charge of the new
school has already been recommended.(Signed Secretary APB) Note from
Department Education - The enrolment according to verbal information
received is about 25. The building has been provided by the A.P Board,
and the teacher is actually in charge of the school , The route for
forwarding materials is rail to Casino. Recommended that formal approval
be given for the establishment of the school for aboriginals at Tabulum.

Memo to Department of Lands from Department of Education -- 3/5/1938 --
"Approval has been given for the establishment of an aboriginal
Provisional school at Tabulum. A building has been erected by the
Aborigines Protection Board on the Aborigines Reserve at Tabulum. Kindly
advise me whether you have any objection to this school being known as
the "Tabulum Aboriginal School."

Department of Lands -- 3/5/38 -- No objection to the designation
referred to -- Tabulum Aboriginal

TABULUM -- Post Town -- Tabulum District -- Casino

Establishment of Aboriginal Provisional School approved. R.P.
22.3.38/27507.

**[Teachers]**

Inspection Report -- 17/5/1938 -- Mr. Brealey has recently been
appointed to Tabulum Aborigines School, and has not yet been able to
rent a residence. With regard to his application for variation of time
and (......) routine I suggest the following (1) School to start at 8:30
AM and end at 2:30 PM. Allowing 1 hour for dinner. (2) If opportunity
cannot yet be found for the manual training and Gardening. I suggest
that the additional time be given to the senior pupils who have had, as
yet, little or no schooling.

Application for Sewing Allowance -- Brearely. Number of girls eligible
to receive tuition. Teachers classification -- nil. Memo from Department
of Education 22/8/1938 Recommending that Mrs. Brearley be appointed as
Teacher of Sewing at Tabulum for 4 hours weekly, and that she be paid
sewing allowance at the rate of 2/6 per hour. - Approved

**[Curriculum and Resources]**

4/5/2938 -- "Dear Sir, I beg to advise you that a copy of the revised
Syllabus for Aboriginal Schools and the General Syllabus has come to
hand. Regarding the hours of lessons. I wish to draw your attention to
some children residing about five miles away from the school. The
parents of these children say it is very difficult for their children to
be at school be 8:30 AM and say 9:00 AM is the best they can do. As
these children are from 10 to 16 years of age, and have not attended a
School previously, their full attendance is desirable. Would you please
let me know if the time of commencement mentioned in the Syllabus viz.
8:30 AM should be altered to 9:00AM to meet the difficulty. With
reference to the time on the Syllabus for the Dinner interval. The
distance from the village to the Reserve is 3 miles by the Cart Track.
There is a shorter foot-path on the hill side, which however, can only
be used on a horse when the path is wet or greasy. While I am residing
at this Hotel I can get Dinner between 1PM and 2PM only. The interval
mentioned in the Syllabus viz. 11:30 AM to 1:30 PM will necessitate my
going without food from morning until night, unless I take lunch and
milk with me, for which a charge of 1/3 per day would be made. I would
request that if the Dinner interval mentioned in the Syllabus be adhered
to, an allowance be granted for the cost of lunch. I would also request
instructions regarding manual training, needlework and Domestic Science.
I understand that the Police Officer has written for some material for
junior manual work and for gardening tools, but in the absence of any
tools or facilities for carpentry for the elder boys, or needlework and
cookery for the girls, will you please give me instructions as to the
manner in which the time given in the Syllabus for these subjects is to
be occupied. There are 23 children on the Roll at present, with an
average daily attendance of about 20. With one exception, some of the
children have been to School before. All of them are, therefore, in
First Class. (Signed by teacher Bearley) To district Inspector of
Schools.

To Local Inspector of Schools -- 1/8/1938 -- Dear Sir, I wish to inform
you that I have now joined my husband at Tabulum and request
instructions regarding the needlework and Home Management classes
referred to in the new Syllabus for Aboriginal Schools. Will you please
let me know if it is intended that the Home Management classes should be
held at my home. There is not any accommodation in the Reserve. --
Department requested that a list showing name and age of girls attending
the school be submitted.

Name list of children sent to Department. 14 girls listed.

Suggested that Mrs. Brearly be asked to teach sewing at the school under
usual conditions but that any arrangements for Home Management be
postponed till I visit the school. I am taking if for granted that Mrs.
Brearly is appointed as teacher only not as teacher -- manager 13/8/38
