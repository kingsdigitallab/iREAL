**Role of the Department and relationship to the APB**

4/7/1903 -- Memorandum to Chief Inspector Dept. Education --
recommending the establishment of a Provisional School for Aboriginal
children at Nulla Nulla Creek near Bellbrook Macleay River. "During my
tour of Inspection of Schools on the Upper Macleay I visited the
Government Reserve at Nulla Nulla Creek and I beg to report as follows:
About 100 Aborigines (including infants) live on the Reserve. About 60
p.c of these are there continually, the remainder being out working up
or down the river. There are 9 families with children between the ages
of 4 and 14, the number in each family ranging from 1 to 4, the total
number of children between these ages being 19. A list of these children
is forwarded herewith. There are also 8 children on the Reserve under 4
years. If a school were established at the place I believe the average
attendance of 12 could be maintained for some time. Several buildings
are now being erected as dwellings for the aborigines, the Ab.
Protection Board having granted the necessary building materials. A good
site for a school can be found on the reserve, and the cost of a
suitable building I estimate as follows: ..."

Letter concerning this matter sent on to the APB

The Aborigines Protection Board have approved of the sum of £20 being
allowed for the purpose of providing a building for the proposed School
for Aborigines at Nulla Nulla Creek, and asking this Department
(Education) to take the necessary action in this matter.

Memoranda to the Chief Inspector -- Nulla Nulla Creek (Aborig) Erection
of building 16/1/1904 -- Recommending that the contractor be paid for
the work having been undertaken for the erection of the school building.
Department of Education recommending that money be paid by the APB.

Name list of children attached who are living on the Government Reserve
