**Role of the Department and relationship to the APB**

5/12/1928 -- Dept. Education to APB re establishment of School at
Nowendoc -- "I desire to inform you that Mr. G. D. Martin, Inspector of
Schools, Tamworth, has reported that at Nowendoc there are 19 children
of school age who are at present without schooling. All the children are
either half or quarter cast aborigines and it is reported that the
Nowendoc Hall is available and would be suitable accommodation. I shall
be glad to have the opinion of your Board regarding the education of
these children." Note from Officer in Charge of Police NOWENDOC --
"Referred to report regarding the Aborigines residing in the district
and their degree of permanency." James Mitchell, Chairman AP Board per
Inspector of Police ARMIDALE -- "I beg to report that on the occasion
that Mr. Martin visited Nowendoc, I saw his last of names of the
children, they are all half or Quarter Caste with the exception of three
of four. They are all permanent residents of Nowendoc, most their
parents being land holders." James E. Gilroy Contstable Nowendoc.

24/12/1928 - Police Department Armidale -- re establishment of school at
Nowendoc -- "If these children are light colored and living in decent
homes -- why are they not admitted to the Public School -- They are in
most places. I would like to visit the place and get particulars."
Signed R.D

22/1/1929 -- Dept. Education to APB re establishment of School at
Nowendoc -- "In connection with the above matter this Department is in
receipt of communications urging that the matter be expedited. I shall
be glad to learn if a decision has been reached by your Board regarding
any communication above mentioned."

30/1/1929 -- Dept. Education re Establishment of School at Nowendoc "In
connection with the above I beg to advise that arrangements are being
made for Inspector Donaldson of my Board to visit Nowendoc in the
immediate future, with a view to enquiring into the advisableness of
establishing a school for Aborigines there."

22/3/1929 -- APB to Dept. Education re Establishment of Aborigines
School at Nowendoc -- "In December last the Education Department advised
that its District Inspector at Tamworth had reported that there were
nineteen children of Aboriginal caste residing at Nowendoc, who were
without schooling, and asked whether the Board would favour the
Department with its opinion in the matter. Inspector Donaldson has
visited the locality and his report is attached."

Report -- Feb 6^th^ 1929 -- "46 Miles SE of Walcha. No Reserve -- Merely
a Police Station and public Hall. -- Within 3 1/2 miles there are 5
families with an admixture of aborigine blood -- 1 Man, James Barr, is a
H.C, all the others are Quadroon or Octoroon. 3 of them are landowners
-- holding 3000, 1680 and 1470 acres respectively. They rear some cattle
and make big money in winter tracking rabbits -- the skins of that
season fetching 1/- each. They have 18 children of school age. They are
most anxious to have them educated. Owing to true unpleasantness -- the
school -- then a subsidized one -- was closed. The parents of the white
pupils objecting to the darker ones attending. This occurred about 2
years ago. ..... Pupils -- besides the colored children there are 2
white families who will send their children. The Wilders live 2 1/2
miles away and have 4 of school age and the Margeys about 1/2 mile away
have 3 to send. Hall is 30' \* 19' \* 12' walls in good order. It has 4
doors, 5 large windows, a heating stove and abundance of school
furniture and material. It is ready for immediate occupation. Constable
Gilroy said there was a rumor that a section of the Trustees was hostile
to letting it for an Aborigines School on account of alleged consumption
amongst them -- the abos. The Halls is used for meetings and dancing. If
a young lady is appointed, Mrs Gilroy, wife of the Constable, will Board
her. She would be within 200 years of the hall and living in 1^st^ class
quarters. As there are no children in the family, Mrs Gilroy would
appreciate the company of a young lady. I recommend the appointment by
made as early as possible as the parents of the children are very
anxious to have a start made. They have been waiting a couple of years
for it. Although in an isolated position, it is a very attractive place
and teacher will have plenty of company and bush recreation."

APB to Dept. Education -- 27/3/1929 Re establishment of Aborigines
School at Nowendoc "The attached papers are, by direction of my Board,
forwarded to your Department for favour of consideration. Under the
circumstances it is recommended that the establishment of this school be
approved. Should such approval be given I attach hereto an application
by Miss Margaret Kinnear of Forster, who was previously teaching an
Aborigines School and might be pleased to accept the appointment at
Nowendoc if offered to her." Signed AC Pettit Secretary

9/5/1929 -- Dept. Education to APB re Establishment of School at
Nowendoc "Adverting to your letter of 27^th^ march last (No. 27/20484)
and previous correspondence, I have to inform you that approval has been
given for the establishment of an Aborigines School at Nowendoc,
conditional upon your Board paying the rental of the Hall in which
school will be held, and that no objections will be raised to the
enrolment and attendance of white children at the school. In May last
year an application by Miss. M. Kinnear of Forster, for appointment as
Teacher of this School was forwarded by you (No.27/19245), and if Miss.
Kinnear still desires to be considered for the position she might be
asked to complete the enclosed copy of the prescribed form and return it
to this Department."

17/6/1929 -- Dept. Education Stores Department -- Approval has been
given ..... for the establishment of an Aborigines Provisional School at
Nowendoc. The school will be conducted in a local hall. Nineteen
children are available for enrolment. Please arrange for the dispatch of
a "first supply" of materials for this number. The route is: - Rail to
Walcha Road."'

17/6/1929 -- Dept. Education to Mr. J. W. Barr Nowendoc \_ "Dear Sir, I
desire to inform you that the establishment of an Aborigines Provisional
School at Nowendoc has been approved by the Minister for Education. No
objection will be raised to the attendance of white children. The school
will be conducted in the Nowendoc Hall. A teacher will be appointed as
soon as possible."

25/5/1928 Letter from M. Kinnear to Dept. Education "Dear Sir, I have
heard that resignation from the Aborigines School at Purfleet is about
to be tendered by the teacher-in-charge. In the event of this being
correct will you please advise your committee that I am still willing to
accept an appointment to the position. Further reference to myself you
will find in your records. I was engaged as a teacher in Aborigines
school from April 1912 till March 1923 being retrenched in 1923 at

1/6/1928 -- Letter from M. Kinnear to Dep. Education re application for

15/4/1931 -- Letter from Inspector re Miss M. Kinnear "Miss Margaret
Kinnear -- temporary teacher in charge of Nowendoc Aboriginal
Provisional School is doing most commendable work in that very isolated
locality. Her attention to duty is exemplary and her effectiveness as
teacher very evident. Her services should be retained by the Education
Department."

14/11/1931 -- Teacher Kinnear applying for permission to close school
earlier to travel by car to the nearest railway at Walcha fifty-four
miles distant. "The pupils would not be losing any time as they always
work at least fifteen minutes extra time each school day and we did not
close for eight hour day, as there were no festivities here on that
date."' Note from Department. "This request might be declined" Letter
sent in reply to Teacher "I have to inform you that your request
abovementioned cannot be acceded to. Please sign and forward this
memorandum though the usual channel to your Inspector."

30/6/1930 -- Memo from Teacher re School closed on account of Flood
"Sir, I have the honor to ask for leave of absence from 16^th^ instant
to 20^th^. The river became flooded on the night of the 15^th^ (Sunday)
and on Monday morning was so dangerous and uncrossable. The pupils were
able to come from their homes to school on Monday, but I called them to
the river bank and dismissed them for the day, after having made several
attempts to cross over to them. On Tuesday the flood increased so much,
that the children were unable to come to school, as the two creeks
between their homes and school were flooded and uncrossable. The flood
has no subsided. I re-commenced duty today 23^rd^ instant. Being cut
from communication I regret being unable to advise you of these
circumstances sooner." Approved.

8/10/1928 - Letter to Inspector of Schools Dept. Education from the
Nowendoc Hall Trustees. "Dear Sir, With regard to your letter re
Nowendoc Public Hall for School Purposes and Rental. I placed the letter
before the meeting of the Trustees, and I have been instructed to inform
you that the Hall will be available for school purposes at the Rental
previously offered by Dept. Education namely £26 per annum. I may state
that all the improvements required by the Dept. were carried out by the
Trustees namely lining and ceiling and installing a heating stove.
Unfortunately at that time the School was broken up for want of
attendance. I am enclosing a letter from Dept. at that time with regard
to the matter.

30/8/1929 -- Letter from Dept. Education to Mr. Alex Watson, Hon.
Secretary, Nowendoc Hall Trustees, Nowendoc WALCHA -- "Dear Sir, In
reply to your letter of the 16^th^ instant addressed to Mr. Inspector
Martin relative to the occupation of the Nowendoc Hall for school
purposes and he payment of rental, I desire to advise that this
Department approved of the establishment of an aborigines' school at
this centre on the condition that the Board for the Protection of
Aborigines agreed to pay rental of the hall and on the understanding
that no objection would be raised to the attendance of white children. I
have to request, therefore, that you communicate with the Secretary of
this Board, at No. 5, Richmond Terrace, Sydney, regarding the suggested
agreement between the Hall Trustees and the Board as to the occupation
of the Hall for school purposes."
