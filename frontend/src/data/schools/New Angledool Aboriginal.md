18/7/1913 -- Letter from Teacher R. A. Nossiter re application for
"School Bank" -- "Dear Sir, Several of my scholars have requested me to
open a "School Bank". As it would be a distinct forward step for them,
and it will be a standing incentive to others, might I suggest that
their request be granted." Chief Inspector: I beg to recommend this
application. Additional note: One teacher only. Application declined.

10/7/1913 -- APB to Dept. Education re Establishment of a School Savings
Bank -- Desiring. "Sir, I have the honor, by direction, to inform you
that I am in receipt of a communication from the Teacher and Manager of
the Aboriginal Station, New Angledool, in which he states that he
desires to inaugurate a Savings Bank system for the benefit of the
children on the Station. He has accordingly been instructed that the
money deposited with him is to be lodged to the joint credit of a Member
of the Local Aborigines Protection Committee and himself at the branch
of the Government Savings Bank at that place, and to issue and secure
receipts for all monies received or paid out. It is suggested that the
District School Inspector might audit the account when making his annual
inspection of the School and I should be pleased if arrangements could
be made with him accordingly." Signed AC Pettitt pro Secretary AP Board.
Note to the Chief Inspector "This is a one teacher school and under the
rules we would not establish a Public School Savings Bank thereat. The
Bank has been opened on the approval of the Aboriginal Protection Board,
not as a School Bank, and the matter of the audit by an Inspector of
Schools is open for your decision."

28/7/1914 -- Dept. Education to APB "Sir, With reference to your letter
dated 10^th^ instant respecting the establishment of a Savings Bank at
the Aboriginal Station at New Angledool, and requesting that the local
Inspector of Schools might audit the account when making his annual
inspection of the school. I have to acquaint you that your request has
been complied with. The Inspector has been asked to make arrangements
accordingly."

9/4/1915 -- Memo Dept. Education -- New Angledool Aborigines School --
Appointment of Teacher -- "Under Secretary, Owing to the transfer of Mr.
Nossiter a vacancy exists at the above school. I recommend that Mr.
George S. Evans be appointed as Temporary Teacher on the understanding
that he will have no claim to any other position under this Department
-- with payment at the rate of £110 per annum together with Living
Allowance. Mr Evans is the nominee of the Aborigines Protection Board
for this position."

24/3/1914 -- APB to Dept. Education Re Appointment of Mr. R. A. Nossiter
as Teacher of the Aboriginal School at Roseby Park -- "The Department of
Public Instruction advise that the transfer of Mr. R. A. Nossitter from
the Aborigines School at New Angledool to Roseby Park has been approved,
and he has been directed to take charge from the 19^th^ January 1914.
The Department desire the Board to nominate a successor for New
Angledool. An application has been received from a Mr. J. W. Holmes for
the vacancy at Angledool, who states that he has had a good public
school education under Mr. J. W. Turner, and that the understands
Aborigines and can handle stock. He is anxious to be informed of the
result of his application as early as possible. Mr. Holmes was an
unsuccessful applicant for the recent vacancy at Brewarrina." Note at
bottom "Having interviewed a number of applicants for the above
position, my Board unanimously recommend the appointment of Mr. George
S. Evans, and would be glad if he be instructed to take up duty as early
as possible"

APN 26/3/1914 -- Vacancies for Teacher and Manager at Angledool, and
Teacher and Manager at Nymoida Aboriginal Stations "We beg to report,
for the Boards information, that in response to the advertisement
calling for applications for the position of Teacher and Manger of an
Aboriginal Station, thirty six (36) applicants were received, and after
careful consideration of the same, thirteen (13) of the applicants were
invited to interview the Committee. Nine (9) of those communicated with
presented themselves, and were personally interviewed regarding their
qualifications, and we have no hesitation in recommending the
appointment of Mr. George F. Evans of "The Avenue", Blackheath as
Teacher and Manager at Angledool, and Mr. Milton C. Newnham as Teacher
and Manger at Nymbodia." Signed (Sgd) G. E. ARDILL Vice Chairman and
(Sgd) T. GARVIN Member of Board.

5/12/1915 -- Telegram to APB from Manager Foster "Regret Nymbodia too
indefinite if anything definite or any suitable appointment to offer
will reconsider if not desire resignation be accepted."

1/9/1916 -- Memo to APB from Manager Foster at Angledool "Managers
resignation "Sir, I herewith tender my resignation as Manager and
Teacher of the Aborigines Station, New Angledool. One months notice is
given as from the 1^st^ instant. The Manager also respectfully desires
to ask the Board for a reference in connection with his (nearly) 7
(seven) years service. 2 (two) years of this period in dual capacity of
Manager and Teacher. I have been compelled to arrive at this decision
owing to my partial removal 6 (six) months ago."

7/9/1916 -- APB to Foster Manager New Angledool "Dear Sir, Please note
that I have received this day a report from the Officer in charge of
Police at Nymbodia to the effect that 5 full blood Aborigines have
returned to the Station, and that several others who are employed in the
district will, in the opinion of the Police also return upon the
completion of their work. Of course, such action on their part may not
eventuate for some time, but it occurred to me that seeing that there
now appears to be some prospect at Nymbodia again warranting the
appointment of a Teacher at some future date you might desire to
reconsider the matter of your resignation, which I intended placing
before the Board at its next meeting on Thursday next, the 14^th^
instant. You might wire me as to whether you still desire that your
resignation be accepted, or whether you would be willing to wait some
time longer for developments."

14/9/1916 \_ APB -- "Board meeting 18/9/1916 -- Submitted. The Board, as
previously constituted approved of Mr. Forster's transfer to Nymbodia in
the hope that his presence there would induce the Aborigines to return
to the Station. Mr. Forster was informed accordingly. When the new Board
had been appointed, however I resubmitted the matter to them, with a
recommendation that Mr. Foster's transfer to Nymbodia be allowed to
stand over until it could be seen whether the Aborigines would return to
that place. To date not one Aborigine has returned, and no other vacancy
exists to which Mr. Foster could be transferred. He is aware of all the
facts, and stated some months ago that in the event of the Board being
unable to transfer him he would resign. Under these circumstances, I see
no alternative but to now accept his resignation." AC Pettit Secretary
APB. Additional note. "It was decided to accept Mr. Foster's
resignation, and to furnish him with a suitable reference. It will now
be necessary to consider the question of filling the vacancy thus
created."

4/2/1921 -- APB to Dept. Education re Transfer of Mr. S. C. Gibson from
position of Teacher -- Manager, Aboriginal Station, Angledool, to
position of Manager, Aboriginal Station Cumeroogunga. -- "I am directed
to inform you that at the last meeting of the Aborigines Protection

2/11/1932 -- APB to Dept. Education Re Transfer of Mr. F. C. Cavill from
position of Teacher, Aborigines School, New Angledool to similar
position at Gingie Gingie via Walgett "I beg to advise that at a special
meeting of my Board held today, consideration was given to certain
reports concerning Mr. F. C. Cavill, Teacher -- Manager, Aboriginal
Station, New Angledool wherein it was disclosed that Mr. Cavill had,
contrary to specific instructions, been having financial transactions
with Aborigines, by disposing to them of certain goods, and accepting
payment there for, and it was decided to recommend to your Department
that he be transferred from his present position to that of teacher only
of the Aborigines School at Gingie Gingie via Walgett, and the present
Teacher there, Miss M. McAulay, be transferred to an existing vacancy at
Pooncarie. Applications are being invited through the press to fill the
vacancy thus created at Angledool, and a recommendation concerning these
will be forwarded to your Department in due course. Mr. Cavill is being
informed of the action being taken in this case."

26/5/1936 -- APB to Dept. Education Re Temporary transfer of Mrs. N. C.
Clark from Angledool to Toomelah via Boomi. "I beg to advise that owing
to a sudden outbreak of serious eye infection upon the Aboriginal
Station at Toomelah, via Boomi, and at the request of the Director
General of Public Health, Mrs. N. C. Clark, Teacher of the Aborigines
School at Angledool was transferred on the 26^th^. Instant to the
Aboriginal Station at Toomelah, to take charge of the treatment of
aboriginal patients in her capacity as Matron, she being also a trained
nurse, the Board being anxious to avail themselves of her services. I
might add that owing to the merging of Angledool Station with
Brewarrina, and the transfer of all the Aborigines to the latter place,
the Aborigines School at Angledool was permanently closed on the date
mentioned. Pending the return of Mrs. Clark to Brewarrina, where she
will be Assistant -- Teacher, the Teacher-in-Charge, Mr. R. R. Brain
will carry on the School."

6/12/1932 -- APB to Department of Education re Recommended Appointment

30/10/1915 - Memo from Teacher to Dept re Application for position of
Temporary Teacher -- "Sir, I have the honor to address you on the above
matter. I have been engaged for the past five years as a Teacher of

the Department. I beg to ask for a position as a temporary teacher. In

Note added on bottom C.I "As stated, in my report on Mr. Fosters School.

strongly recommend Mr. Forster's application for most favourable

House." Signed W.G.H

3/10/1916 -- Letter from H. J. Brett to APB re application for
employment "Sir, I have the honour to reply to your favour of Sept.
25^th^ re Teacher Managers position at Angledool and beg to submit my
application for same. I have been in New South Wales for a period of 2
1/2 years and have spent the last part of that time in the employ of
Silvester Bros. Redfern. My term of engagement with this form was broken
and my joining the Expeditionary Forces but I resumed duties there
shortly after obtaining my discharge. Previous to coming to this State,
I was in Durham S. A for 2 years under my Uncle who was in charge of
native labor for J. L Bennie Son and Co in that Town. I am 22 years of
age, of good physique, fair education, and have a good all-round general
knowledge, trusting this may have your consideration as I am fully
confident that I could undertake the duties. Enclosed find copies of
references which I hold, and I have already written to Durham for
others."

25/9/1916 -- Reply from APB to Mr. Brett -- "Sir, with reference to your
interview with me at this office, I beg to inform you that a vacancy for
a Temporary Teacher-Manager will occur at the end of this month on the
Aboriginal Station at Angledool. As explained, the position may be only
a temporary one as the Department at present have two of its officers at
the Front whose positions must of course be preserved on them. The one
under notice now carries with it a salary from the Education Department
of £110 per annum, plus Expensive District Allowance of I think £20 per
annum. The allowance paid by the Board are £12 for self as Manager, and
£12 for your wife as Matron, together with rations and residence. If you
care to submit an application, the mater will receive consideration.
Copies of testimonials should of course be forwarded at the same time."
Signed AC Pettit Secretary

13/11/1916 -- Letter from Dept. Education to Mr. Brett re appointment as

6/1/1928 -- Letter from Teacher Wilkins to Dept. Education re cleaning
allowance cheque -- "I am enclosing my cheque which according to my best
knowledge of allowance is £1 -- short. You have the etc of my school.
The native children live in dirty surroundings, that I've to daily order
to be "cleaned up". Every child is washed "at school" even then they
know no pride of school room. The grounds are dreadful for weeks after
rain and drought, we have a "daily red dust"' storm. Apart from the
daily cleaning and dusting of school room -- I scrub it out floor walls
desk forms and out houses once per week and mop it out, also once per
week. Inspectors, visitors all say how beautifully clean it always is.
Yes, at what cost! !! -- If I am unable from any cause to do it myself
I've to pay for it to be done. A dark woman charges 4/- a white woman
5/- 1s each is given to the two children whose turn it is to do daily
sweep up and in drought time when water for 7 months lasts year had to
be carried and bought. It cost me another 1/-. I average always 2/- per
week, for help in keeping the school clean. It needs more cleaning than
a White school as White children are of clean habits dark children are
of dirty habits. The temperature in the school room goes from 100 to 110
and cleanliness is essential. Yes! But at 110 I'm often scrubbing the
floors and desks. Its unfair, other teachers with much easier
surroundings are better paid. I work hard no leaving off until duty
done. In broiling heat and get less all the way around. No dark woman
would take on that school cleaning for £1.16.0 for 6 mths and I
certainly wont have away at it, in the heat, some consideration must be
shown. I have confidence, in my "heads" to know a "Cause" stated will be
considered."

20/3/1923 - Reply to teacher Wilkins from Dept. Education "With
reference to your request for payment of an increased cleaning
allowance, it is regretted that there is no provision for payment of a
higher rate than that now being payed in the case of the school under
your charge."

25/9/1928 -- Dept. Education School Medical Service to the Chief
Inspector: Subject New Angledool Aboriginal School "The following copy
of a report regarding the above school, received from the School Dental
Officer who is at present working in the district, is forwarded for your
information: - "I should like to mention the very excellent conditions
found at this school. The school building is one of the cleanest and
best that I have ever seen. The children are also remarkably clean and
well cared for, special attention being paid to health conditions.
Dentally all children were seen and practically all required some form
of treatment, at the same time the mouths were all reasonably clean and
well cared for. I think great credit is due to Mr. Milne, who is in
charge, for his interest and efforts in handling children of this
difficult type." Signed Harvey Sutton Principal Medical Officer.
Additional note added by Department "Send Copy of report to the
Secretary AP Board."
