**Role of the Department and relationship to the APB**

9 June 1920 APB to Dept Education -- re supply of furniture and material
for School, Aborigines Home, Singleton "I am advised by the
Superintendent of the Aborigines Home at Singleton that a case of School
material and a Blackboard and Easel have been received. However, no
furniture is yet to hand and as it is anticipated that the School will
be opened at an early date, I shall be glad if you will arrange for same
to be expedited. Thanking you in anticipation. A Pettitt Secretary" Note
from Department "No definite rule appears to have been set up with
regard to supplying furniture to Aboriginal Schools. From what I can
gather in some case the Aborigines Protection Board either supplies or
pays for the furniture in others it has been supplied by the Department.
A definite rule should be established. It might be mentioned that it is
the practice of the Aborigines Protection Board to provide school
buildings and for the Department to supply a limited quantity of school
materials. This Department will supply the necessary furniture.
Ascertain what is necessary." (19.7.1920)

29 June 1920 -- Letter from Mr. Thomas Austin to Inspector Back at
Muswellbrook asking for school furniture. "Dear Sir, I have to inform
you that I have taken charge of the above school, as from 28/6/20. There
is no furniture in the school yet. I understand that forms, desks,
press, chair and table were ordered about three months ago, by Mr.
Lewis, Principal of the Singleton School, in conjunction with Sgt
Menary. It is impossible to do any indoor school work under the
circumstances, but I am devoting school hours to outside work, such as
gardening and general improvements." Note from Chief Inspector "I would
strongly urge that supply of furniture be expedited."

Memo from the Supt. Aborigines Home Singleton to The Secretary,
Aborigines Protection Board. Subject: A. Non arrival of school desks B.
Table, chair, and press for school. "Dear Sir, I have to inform you that
the desks forms for school use have not arrived here yet. Seeing that
the school re-opens on June 28^th^ it will be necessary for these items
to be delivered during the coming week. It will also be necessary to
supply a chair and table for teachers use, and a press for the books, as
there is nothing in the school at present. I shall be glad if you will
make arrangements for the early delivery of these goods." Thomas H.
Austin

Letter from Teacher Mr. Thomas Austin to Inspector of Schools -- 28 July
1920 -- from the "Aborigines School George Street, Singleton". "Dear
Sir, I have to inform you that the following items are required to
furnish this school : - 6 Desks, 6 forms, 1 table, 1 chair, 1 large
press, 36 Inkwells. It is expected that there will be from 30 to 35 boys
attending this school"

Letter from Dept. Ed to APB 16 August 1920 -- " Singleton Aborigines
School: Supply of Furniture -- I have to inform you that the
Officer-in-Charge of this Department's Furniture Workshops has been the
instructed to supply the under mentioned furniture to the Singleton
Aborigines' School as a matter of special urgency. 6 desks and forms 8'4
in length, 6 forms, 1 small table, 1 chair and 1 large desk."

6 October APB to Dept. Education re Supply of furniture to School at
Aboriginal Boys Home, Singleton "In the above communication and from
your Department it was intimated that Desks and forms, table, chair,
etc, were being forwarded to the Aboriginal Boys Home, Singleton, as a
matter of special urgency. However, I am in receipt of a communication
from the Superintendent of the Institution to the effect that up to the
present of the furniture has not been received. Under the circumstances,
I shall be glad if you will cause inquiries to be made, and the goods
forwarded without delay, in order that the School may be opened."

Mr. Thomas Austin receiving goods sent on the Government railways at the
Aboriginal school Singleton 13 October 1920.

January 12 1924 - Memo re Aborigines Boys Home School Singleton Class
6^th^ -- (1) Closing of Aborigines Boys home School (II) Transfer of
material to Aborigines School Brungle via Gundagai "Dear Sir, I have
received instructions from the Secretary of the Aborigines Protection
Board, to close this institution on January 15 inst. I have been given
six weeks leave and instructed to take up duty as Teacher Manager at the
Aborigines Station, Brungle via Gundagai, on Deb, 28^th^ instant. The
Secretary of the Aborigines Protection Board has advised the Education
Dept. of these arrangements. There will be ten boys from here to be sent
to Brungle. All stores and material have been forwarded to Brungle and I
packed all reading books and writing material and one press with them. I
am leaving in the school 6 desks and 6 forms, 1 table, 1 press, 2
Black-boards." Note "Furniture is the property of the Education
Department. Buildings and site belong to the Aborigines Protection
Board"

**Teachers**

24 July 1920 -- APB to Dept. Education -- "Appointment of Mr. T.H.
Austin, Late teacher Aborigines School, Mount Olive, as Superintendent,
Aborigines Boys Home, Singleton." I beg to inform you that at the last
Meeting of my Board, held on the 14^th^ inst. The Salary of Mr. T. H.
Austin, later teacher Manager of the Aboriginal Station, Mount Olive,
and now Teacher-Superintendent of the Aborigines Home, Singleton, was
increased to £25 (twenty-five pounds) per annum."

**Curriculum and Resources**

4 Dec 1920 -- Memo from Aborigines Boy Home, Singleton re "application
for bench, tools, materials for light wood work -- I have the honor to
make application for the under mentioned items for teaching light
wood-work. 1 Teachers bench -- B Type. 1 Set tools for woodwork --
ordinary woodwork. 6 sets tools for boys -- ordinary woodwork. Supply of
material -- timber. Eleven (11) boys will be taking a course of
instruction. With reference to my own qualifications I would point out
that I have instruction in wood-work and technical drawing at the Simon
Langton Boys School England. I was also teaching wood-work at the
Northfleet Council set: England for nearly two years. I have been
teaching the subject at Mt Olive for four years. Submitted for your
information." Note from inspector I recommend that tools, bench, and
timber be supplied at as early date as possible."

14 March 1921 -- Letter to APB from Dept. Ed re Application by the
Teacher of the Aborigines Boys' Home Public School, Singleton, for the
supply of Manual Training Material. "The Teacher of the Aborigines Boys'
Home Public School, Singleton, (Mr. T. H. Austin), has made application
for the supply of one woodwork bench, tools and timber for Manual
Training purposes. Mr. Austin has been teaching woodwork at Mt. Olive
for four years. Her formerly taught the subject for two years at the
Norfleet Council School, England. Eleven boys will benefit by the
instruction. As this Department has no funds from which this material
could be supplied, it is thought that perhaps your Board might be able
to find the money required for the purchase of this material."
