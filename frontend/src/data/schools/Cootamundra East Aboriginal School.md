29/1/1913 -- Dept. Lands concerning adoption of name of school. "Sir, In
reply to your letter of the 15^th^ January, 1913, I have the honor to
inform you that there is no objection to the use of the name
"Cootamundra East Aborigines" for the provisional school to be
established at Cootamundra East. The name will be noted as adopted on
the lists of Public Schools in this Department."

25/1/1913 -- APB to Dept. Education re Cootamundra East Aborigines
Provisional School -- Referred to the Under Secretary, Department of
Public Instruction, who will, perhaps, be good enough to have the
necessary furniture forwarded to Cootamundra as early as convenient."

25/9/1929 -- Memo from Teacher to Dept. Education re forthcoming picnics
"Sir, The Matron has informed me that it is usual for the children at
the Home to receive invitations for three separate picnics -- one from
the Church of England, one from the Presbyterians, and one from the
Methodists. The children in the past years have attended all three
picnics. According to regulations, only one day each year is allowed fro
children to attend a picnic. Have I your permission to close the school
on three different days if the picnics should occur on school days." E.
M. Higgins Teacher.

Note to the Chief Inspector from Local Inspector Ravenscroft "The Chief
Inspector, I am unable to make a recommendation. Please note that this
is a girls' aboriginal school, in which the social aspect is a
particularly important one. It is very improbable that the three picnics
should be held on the one day, and I believe all the girls (20) will be
invited to each one."

Reply from Chief Inspector 1/10/1929 -- PICNICS -- I have to inform you
that the restrictions in regard to the holding of picnics will not apply
to your school and the various invitations might therefore be accepted.
This memorandum should be signed by you and forwarded to your Inspector,
without delay."

1/10/1929 -- Memo Dept. Education -- Cootaumundra East: Picnics --
"Different conditions operate in the case of aboriginal children. The
rule respecting Picnics might be waved and the teacher advised that the
restrictions will not apply to this school and that the invitations
might therefore be accepted."

24/10/1929 -- APB to Dept. Education re Aborigines School, Cootamundra
-- Probable resignation of Mrs. Higgins -- Recommended re-appointment of
Miss A. Wales -- I beg to advise that I am in receipt of information to
the effect that it is the probable intention or Mrs. Higgins to resign
her position as Teacher of the Aborigines School at Cootamundra for the
purposes of proceeding to England early in January next. It is
recommended that the vacancy thus created be filled by the reappointment
of Miss Alice Wales, who retired from the position some time ago, and
has since been employed by Mr. Board as Assistant in the Training Home,
in the grounds of which the School is located. When previously in the
position Miss Wales did wonderful work and it would be greatly to my
Board's advantage that her services by secured in the school. The date
of her birth was 15.10.1929."

Dept. Education DUNOON ABO: Recommending extended leave prior to
retirement in the case of Miss Alice Wales, Teacher. " Miss Wales, who
is in her 60^th^ year, has served continuously for over 16years. She has
applied for the long service leave for which she is eligible from the
abovementioned date at the expiration of which she desires to retire
from the Service in terms of Section 21 of the "Superannuation Act,
1916". She is entitled to 3 months of such leave not having been granted
any previous leave of this nature. Miss Wales might be granted 3 months'
leave from 2^nd^ April to 1^st^ July, 1929 on full pay under Section 13
of the "Public Service (Amendment) Act, 1919" prior to retiring from the
Service at the expiration thereof in terms of Section 21 aforesaid. Last
day of services 1^st^ July, 1929."

29/4/1929 -- APB to Dept. Education - Application by Miss A. Wales,
Teacher of the Aborigines School at Dunoon, Lismore, For Long Service
Leave. I beg to forward the attached application by Miss A. Wales,
Teacher of the Aborigines School at Dunoon via Lismore, for Long Service
Leave, and to strongly recommend same for favourable consideration. Miss
Wales has been in the service of the Department and of my Board for over
sixteen years, during which time she has discharged her duties in a most
efficient and loyal manner, and provided the Regulations will allow is
deserving of any consideration which it might be possible to extend to
her." AC Pettit.

17/4/1930 -- Memo to Dept Education re closing the school on Friday
11^th^ inst. Owing to the teacher going to Sydney on urgent business
with the Secretary A.P. B. "Sir, I have to inform you that This school
was closed last Friday 11^th^ inst. Owing to urgent business with the
Secretary A.P Board Sydney/"

24/4/1930 -- Memo to Dep. Education re teacher wishes to retire after
30^th^ inst. "Sir, Following the Matrons resignation of her position as
Matron of the Girls Training Home. Two weeks ago the Secretary Abor. P.
Board advised her that "my position would be wanted as a result of the
changes being made in the management." Hence my hurried visit to Sydney
on the 11^th^ inst. The Secretary, promised to arrange a transfer for
one as soon as possible. After due consideration I have decided to
retire on my pension as from 30^th^ inst." Signed A. Wales.

1/5/1936 -- Memo from Teacher to Dept. Education re Installation of
Modern desks "The long desks in this school are extremely unhealthy for
the girls as I am forced to sit six and seven in each desk, which, in
some cases it is impossible to make secure to the floor. As these
children are so susceptible to skin diseases I find there would be far
less contagion if they could be separated in dual desks. The room will
only take five long desks comfortably but as I have 34 pupils I am
forced to use six. I had 38 to accommodate last year, and my numbers are
likely to increase at any time. When they do it makes it almost
impossible under the present seating to fit them in." Dual desks
recommended.

27/10/1938 -- APB to Dept. Education -- re Physical Culture for inmates
of Girls' Training Home, Cootamundra -- "The Board's Inspector, Mrs.
English, has reported that on the occasion of her last visit to the
Girls' Training Home, Cootamundrashe learned that the girls are not
receiving any physical instruction at the school and consequently are
greatly lacking in deportment. During the recent procession at
Cootamundra, in which the girls took part, their marching compared most
unfavourably with that of other children. They tend to slouch with heads
held down and appear to have no idea of marching in time. It is
requested that consideration be given to instructions being issued to
the Teacher which will serve to remedy the matter." Signed AC Pettit.

9/12/1938 -- Dept. Education Memo from Inspector to Chief Inspector re
Cootamundra Abor. School -- Physical Training. "I have this day visited
Cootamundra Abor. School and have seen the physical Training. The work
is very fair. I saw all phases including exercises, games, and marching.
Miss Halman follows the London Board Book very closely. I demonstrated
some additional exercises having for their aim to the development of
greater alertness. In the marching there were no signs of "slouching"
movement as stated by Mrs. English. The big girls play basket ball. The
procession referred to in your attached memo was the Coronation
Celebration held in April last -- by no means recent. \] The trouble is
that Miss. Holman and Miss. Nance, the Matron, are not friendly. Mrs.
English apparently hears only one viewpoint."

Sent to Secretary APB 9/1/1939
