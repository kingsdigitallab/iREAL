**Role of the Department and relationship to the APB**

3/7/1907 -- Dept Public Instruction -- Memo to Chief Inspector re Morcom
(Aboriginal) Public Question of Closing. "The enrolment at this school
is 10. and the average attendance 6.3 for the present quarter. I
recommend that the school be closed and the Teacher Mr. Hang be
appointed to Meeting Ridge Public School which is at present closed."
Submitted and approved. APB to be informed.

4/11/1907 -- Memo to Mr. Inspector Kennedy from Chief Inspector J.
Dawson re Morcom Lease of School Site. "The lease of Mr. D. Bain's land
at Morcom as a school site at peppercorn rental will expire on the
31^st^ instant. The building was erected in 1892 at the cost to this
Department of £35. As this school is not at present in operation, will
you kindly obtain the written consent of Mr. Bain to the removal of the
building any time after the expiration of the lease. Should there be no
likelihood of the school being reopened, a recommendation as to the
disposal of the premises might be made." Note to Chief Inspector "Mr.
Bain's written consent is appended. As the Aborigines are again
collecting on the reserve an application for the reopening of the school
may be early expected. It would therefore not be wise to dispose of the
building."

24/2/1908 -- Morcom and Bril Bril -- Question of re - opening. "When in
the Hastings Circuit on other business I took occasion to make inquiries
into the merits of this case and find that there are 15 children of
school age on the Aborigines Reserve at Wauchope (Morcom) and 3 at Bril
Bril. There are however, several families of aborigines scattered about
the Hastings valley. These promise to return either to Morcom or Bril
Bril if the schools are reopened. Not much reliance however can be
placed upon their promises. The distance between the two centres is 18
miles and the working of Half time schools at such distance is
unsatisfactory. The children at Bril Bril are not altogether without the
means of education as the Resident Missionary resides there. Under these
circumstances I think that the best plan would be to re-open Morcom as a
full-time school and the scattered families as well as those at Bril
Bril might be induced to move towards it."

11 June 1908 -- Letter from Mission Station Rolland Plains to the School
Inspector, Kempsey District "Dear Sir, I do not know if you have had nay
report from Mr. Torres of the Morcom Aboriginal School but at that
report -- when you do receive it will be unsatisfactory as regards to
numbers there only being six children of school age there one family of
five children are at Rolland through the influence of local police Serg.
Lawler they are going to the Public School at Port instead of going back
to Wauchope. - At the time that the application was made for the Morcom
and Bril Bril school to be reopened there were only two children here of
school age there are now six with a possible 8 or 9 -- my object -- in
writing to you to ask for consideration of the matter so that the Morcom
instead of being closed should instead be made half time with Bril Bril.
Mr. Torres appears to be getting on well with the children he has a nice
way with them he is a great advance from our last teacher. Having that
there was likely to be a change of Inspector in Kempsey district is the
reason of not addressing this to a name. " Signed Lancelot Railett (??)

1 July 1908 -- Morcom Provisional -- Small attendance threat. "The
quarterly return just received from Morcom Provisional shows an
enrolment of only 6 pupils and the teacher states that these "Will
probably be notified by the owner of the reserve (Mr. Bain) to leave
within a few weeks." The attached letter from Mr. Railton of the Mission
Station suggests that Morcom could be worked HT will Bril Bril. Of the
latter I know nothing -- the name does not occur in any of my papers.
Please have forwarded to me any former papers relating to each of these
places, Morcom and Bril Bril."

2 November 1908 -- Memo to Chief Inspector "The lease of the school site
at Morcom will expire on the 31^st^ December 1908. Will you please
ascertain, if possible, whether there is any probability that the school
will again be required for teaching aboriginal children. When I was on
the Hastings it was reported to me that Mr. Bain had decided not to
allow aboriginals to reside on the reserve. If that is so, the school is
not likely to be again required. Should it be decided to finally close
the school, the building will probably be sold, Please report to me its
condition with an estimate of the value of its material. If you are of
the opinion that it should be sold, perhaps you might be able to obtain
a tender at a reasonable amount for the materials contained in the
building."

12 November 1908 -- Letter from Teacher R. Hopper to Chief Inspector re
Aboriginal School at Morcom "Sir, Mr. Bain has informed me that all the
aborigines must leave his property at Morcom immediately after
Christmas, and that he will not again allow them to settle there. At
present, there are only four huts standing there, and less than half a
dozen children of school age about the place. I believe the building
will not again be required for school purposes. It was erected about
fourteen years ago,. And have never been painted. The timber is decayed
in place. It has a shingle roof. There are two water closets in a very
dilapidated condition. I estimate that, taking the chances of breakages
in dismantling the structure, £10 would be a fair price for the lot. I
enclose Mr. James Bain's tender for the purchase of the building, and I
think it will be difficult to obtain a better offer."

31 December 1908 -- Letter to Dept. Public Instruction forwarding Cheque
of £6 for purchase of old school building from Morcom Aboriginal School

27/6/1908 - Letter sent from Inspector Grafton District, Kempsey Section
to teacher Mr. Torres re Morcom School. "Please send me the information
you can gather regarding the school population at Bril Bril, touching on
the following particulars, 1. Has there been a school at Bril Bril? 2.Is
there a School Building there? 3. What is the possible attendance at a
school there? Give rough plan showing positions of residences. Number of
children of school age and distances from one another and from school
(if any). Mr. Railton (?) of the Mission Station Rollands Plain's could
probably assist you. 4. Show position of Morcom relative to Bril Bril.
Could they be worked together as HT schools? 6. Furnish any other
information likely to be of use in considering the question of working
these two centres as HT.

Letter of reply --

"Sir, In reply to yours of the 27^th^ ultimo, I have the honor to report
as follows. 1. There has been a School at Bril Bril. 2. There is a
school building there Size 17 \* 13; condition fair; equipment black
board and easel, 4 desks, slates etc. 3. I understand from Mr. Railten
(the aborigines missionary) that there are six children who have been
going to school there, hence a possible 6. The parents all live on the
reserve fatherst 1/4 mile from school. 4. Bril Bril is situated 20 miles
north-west of Morcom. 5. I do not think they could be satisfactorily
worked as H.T. schools. Firstly the attendance of both schools (allowing
for irregularity) would probably not exceed eight. Secondly, the
distance apart of these school would render it very inconvenient for a
teacher I have been unable to visit Bril Bril personally, but have
received above information from Mr. Railen and local residents. I have
not shown the positions of the residences at Bril Bril or position of
Morcom relative to Bril Bril, but trust the information supplied will be
all that is required. " Signed Thos A Jones (Teacher)
