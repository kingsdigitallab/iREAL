**Name of School:** Baryulgil Aboriginal School

**School File No:** 5/14817.3

**Date Range of School file:** 1938 - 1939

**Details of School:** prov 11.1938 -- 9.47

**Role of the Department and relationship to the APB**

3/1/1928 - Memo from Department of Education -- recommending that a Tuck
shop will not be suitable for the school.

18/8/1939 "Installation of Shower Bath and heating stove. The Department
arranged for the demolished building at Upper Copmanhurst to be
re-erected at Baryulgil to serve as an Aboriginal school. The teacher
has now asked that a suitable heating stove be installed and that the
shower-room be fitted with a rotary pump and hose to permit of the
children using the room in connection with the physical education
program. Mr Inspecter Gilbert considers that a stove is desirable in the
view of the necessity of inculcating habits of cleanliness in the
children, a shower should be installed as early as possible. The
estimated cost of the showe and pump is £2.10.0. In view of the special
circumstances, it is RECOMMENDED that authority be given for the P & R
staff to install a suitable heating stove an semi-rotary pump complete
with shower at a total cost of £13." Approved by Department.

15/7/1939 -- Memo from Temporary teacher -- Sir, the bathroom at the
school is not yet complete with shower and pump. I have planned to
utilize the shower as part of our physical culture program. We would be
pleased to receive it as soon as possible.

18/7/1939 -- Inspection Report Department of Education -- "I beg to
state that during my inspection of this school I found that a rotary
pump and hose had not been supplied for the shower room which was
erected with the other buildings. As these children have very poor
homes, and cleanliness is not a feature, it is necessary to complete the
school conveniences by the early erection of this shower. I also found
that no heating stove had been erected. This is also necessary. It is
RECOMMENDED that (1) rotary pump, shower and flexible hose be fixed in
the shower and room and (2) that a hearing stove be placed in the
school.

24/7/1939 -- School still had not been supplied with a heating stove.

**Conditions**

> *Students -- conditions of school building*

Painting and repair -- order for erection of building

Provision of building -- Transfer demolished building from Upper
Copmanhurst and re-erect on elected site at Baryulgil. Provide closets.
Type of building suggested is standard classroom 24' \* 21' 6" with
verandah on northern side. Building to be modified to suggestions of
Mr.Inspector Gilbert. Afeter consultation with that Officer. Furniture
and equipment to be transferred with building.
