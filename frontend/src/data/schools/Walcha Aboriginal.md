19/5/1930 -- Letter from George Hoy to Hon. D.H. Drummond, Parliament
House "Dear Sir, with reference to your statement to Mr. Scully re the
closing of Country Schools. I may state that the Summer Vale school was
closed when there was attendance of Eleven (11) children. Some of the
children are now traveling five miles to Walcha School and some are
without education. If this school was reopened I believe the parents
would send their children to the Summervale school instead of Walcha
School as they would be from 200 yards to one mile distant."

4/6/1930 -- Letter from George Hoy to Hon. D.H Drummond, Parliament
House Sydney. "With regards to the opening of the Summervale school. I
have made enquiries from the residents of the locality. They are not in
favour of letting their children leave the Walcha school. So there is
only (5) five children who could attend the school. Had this school not
been closed there would have been (13) thirteen children who could
attending it after Xmas. Let me know what is to be done to give those
children Education, as their parents are not in a position to pay board
away from home to educate them. Nor well enough educated to go on with
the correspondence school."

Dept Education -- 18/8/1930 -- RE: Question of Re-opening school. "The
application for the re-opening of the Summer Vale school was the subject
of a personal investigation by Mr. Inspector Taylor recently. Although
no parents were advised of the intended visit of the Inspector, only
three, representing five children, were present at the meeting. The
others were either indifferent or prefer their children to attend
Walcha, many of whom are in the higher class and, it would be obtained
at a small one-teacher school. So far as white children are concerned
prospects do not warrant opening. An Aborigines school is at present in
operation at Summer Vale in a building which is very unsatisfactory. Its
transfer to the departmental building is recommended on other papers. IT
IS RECOMMENDED that the re-opening of Summer Vale Provisional School be
declined at that the parents advised that there will be no objection to
the enrolment of white children at the Aborigines' school when
transferred to the vested building, but that preference must be given to
coloured children, and that white children can only be admitted so long
as the formation of additional classes is unnecessary. A draft reply is
submitted herewith, for the Minister's signature, if approved."

30 July 1930 -- To the Hon. D.H Drummond, Parliament House Sydney --
Dear Sir, IN your last communication you were going to have an enquiry
made to opening of the SummerVale School. The Inspector was out and
promised to do this best to have it opened as a mixed school for
Aborigines and White children. So far I have heard nothing when the
Aboriginal children were expelled from this school it was overcrowded at
the present time there would be about thirteen (13) Aboriginal and (6)
white children attending which would not overcrowd it. When this school
was closed there was an average attendance of (11) eleven children."

10/7/1930 -- Chief Inspector -- "CI, I visited Summervale today and met
three parents of five children. Parents of other children in the
district had received an invitation to be p resent through the H.M of
Walcha P.S but did not attend. I understand that they are either
indifferent or prefer their children to continue attending walcha P.S,
many of these are in the 6^th^ and higher classes. Prospects as far as
white children are concerned do not in my opinion warrant the reopening
of the school. Accompanying this report recommending the transfer of
Summervale (walcha) Aborigines School to the old building at Summervale.
I RECOMMEND that parents of young children living at Summervale be
informed that their children will be allowed to attend the Aborigines
School transferred to the old building at Summervale provided that
vacancies exist; and that children will not be admitted whose enrolment
will necessitate the formation of additional classes to those in
existence at the Aborigines School. They should also be informed that as
the school is essentially an Aborigines School they must clearly
understand that preference in the matter of enrolment will be extended
to children of Aborigines parents."

9/3/1931 -- Memo from teacher Miss Osborne to Dept. Education - Subject
-- Failure to obtain copy of letter sent to Sergeant of Police from AP
Board r white children attending Walcha Aboriginal School -- "having
applied to the Sergeant of Police, Walcha for a copy of the letter
forwarded to him by the Abo. Protection Board giving permission for
removal of Abo. Children from "Reserve" to Summer Vale school building
also for attendance of white children at same school. I find I am unable
to obtain it. Application will have to be made direct to the AP Board."

20/3/1931 -- Memo from Chief Inspector -- Information sought re
Aborigines School Walcha -- "During the recent inspection of the Walcha
Aboriginal School, the teacher informed me that her instructions were to
teach the four of five whites enrolled only up to the standard reached
by blacks. The Aboriginal School is in a Departmental building situated
at Summer Vale, Walcha. I understand from the teacher that any white
pupils cannot be enrolled should there be too many blacks and preference
must be given to blacks. The teacher, a temporary teacher by name of
Miss. P. Osborne, asked what she would do in this case: - a white girl
fourteen years of age, has failed twice at Walcha Public School to gain
the P.F.E -- she lives at Summer Vale and her mother wishes her to
attend the Summer Vale Aboriginal School. If Miss Osbornes instructions
are ...taught beyond the standard usually teacher by the blacks -- this
girl cannot attend. Miss Osborne stated that these instructions with
regard to being careful not to pass beyond the blacks standard and to
enroll whites only on sufferance were read out to her by the police
sergeant of Walcha. I asked her to secure a copy of instructions from
him. "

25/5/1931 -- Attendance of white children -- Mr. Inspector Cantello,
"Papers in connection with the attendance of white children at Walcha
Aborigines' School are forwarded herewith. It will be noted that in
permitting the attendance of white pupils at this school, the Department
laid it down that preference was to be given to aborigines and the
attendance of white children could be permitted only so long as the
formation of additional classes to those then in existence was not
necessitated. If it is considered desirable to vary this decision, a
recommendation might be made."

**Teachers**

11/11/1931 -- "Walcha Aborigines' School -- Case of Miss Phyllis
Osborne, Teacher -- Under Secretary -- The facts of this case are: -
Miss Osborne was a Short Course student, trained 1928 -- 29, but on the
completion of her College course she was not recommended for appointment
by the Principal, whose report reads: -

> "Miss Osborne is lacking in personality and wanting in confidence as a
> teacher. In her final examination she secured no credits, and failed
> to pass in six subjects, her percentage of marks being 30.7/ Teaching
> ability is assessed at B- to B."

Miss Osborne was accordingly advised, on 4.6.29, that she would not
receive an appointment, and that the Training Bond would not be
enforced. On 31:1:30; Miss Osborne made a formal application for
employment as a 1/3 Small School Teacher, and appeared before the
Employment Committee, but the Committee recommended her for temporary
employment only, and as a Teacher of Aborigines' Schools. She accepted
the position of Teacher of the Aborigines School, Walcha, in February
following, and is still there. In February last Miss Osborne applied for
permission to sit for the Teachers' Examination, but was not allowed to
do so. This was in accordance with Departmental practice, as Teachers of
Aborigines' Schools are not regarded as Departmental Teachers. Miss
Osborne's father, in his letter to the Minister, attached complains that
his daughter has been unjustly treated, but this cannot be admitted. She
is not a Departmental Teacher, having been definitely rejected as such
by reason of her failure as trainee. The father's request, therefore for
a transfer to a Metropolitan School, is out of the question, but it is
open to Miss Osborne to make direct application to the Aborigines
Protection Board if she desires a transfer from Walcha."

Summevale Aborigines -- 15/8/1930 -- Transfer to Vested Building -- "The
Aborigines Protection Board recently requested the transfer of the
Summer Vale Aborigines School from the present building (a church on the
Aborigines Reserve) to the departmental building. It is shown by the
reports of the Board, and the local Inspector, that conditions are very
unsatisfactory at present. Thirteen children are enrolled, and, of
necessity must endevour to write on two desks. There is no press for the
storage of materials, which are placed on the floor. No water is
available at the Church. The children are said to be clean and tidy and
a credit to their parents, who are respectable, and take a keen interest
in the children and the school. The present conditions must be somewhat
disheartening for them. The re-opening of the Summer Vale Provisional
School, dealt with on other papers, is not justified at present, and
there appears to be no reason to object to this proposal to transfer the
school. IT IS RECOMMENDED that the transfer of the Aborigines' School to
the departmental building be approved."

10/9/1930 -- Summer Vale Aborigines School -- Improved Accommodation --
Mr. Inspector Taylor, Will you please submit a report and recommendation
upon this matter. CI. -- Chief Inspector wrote "I visited Walcha
Aborigine School today and found conditions under which children are
working extremely unsatisfactory. Seating and desk accommodation are
bad, no places exist for storing material, and one of the outhouses is,
in my opinion, in a dangerous condition. The teacher's report is a
correct description of the state of affairs. Of the children present,
one is very dark, five a slightly coloured and eight are, as far as
appearance goes, white. Children are clean and with one exception appear
to be free from skin or other diseases. For the most part they appear of
fair mentality and children in the upper part f the school are capable
of doing work in advance of the syllabus for Aborigines Schools. Three
pupils are doing 4^th^ class work of the syllabus for P Schools. I
RECOMMEND that the school be transferred to Summervale and that a
limited number of younger white children be admitted provided that
vacancies exist. Report and recommendation re reopening of Summer Vale
accompanying this."
