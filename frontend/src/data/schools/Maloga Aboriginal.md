23/12/1878 -- Letter from Maloga Aboriginal School to The Secretary
Council of Education, Sydney. Sir, I have the honor to acknowledge the
receipt of your letters of 4^th^ Ultimo and 17^th^ instant -- 78 --
23345. Your former communication I sent on to Mr. Edward Palmer of the
Public Library, Macquarie St, asking him to afford you the required
information, as he is acting Secretary in Sydney for our school. Your
latter communication I send now to John Roseby Esq. M. L. A, who
doubtless will afford all information to the Department respecting the
operations of our Institution. The School at Maloga, which has been in
existence since June 1873 is intended for the education of Aborigines
only, having been erected for that purpose -- at present there are 16
children, and abut an equal number of young married people who receive
instruction from Mr. Matthews and myself. If we had means at our deposit
that might be largely increased, and many children who are exposed to
the wretchedness and degradation of camp life, be led to become
respectable members of society. I have the honor to be Sir, Your most
obedient servant. Daniel Matthews.

1879 -- Memorandum of Inspector to the Secretary -- Maloga Aboriginal
Mission Application for and to (?) Provisional there "I have the honor
to State, for the information of the Council, that I visited the above
locality on the 19^th^ inst, and Lake Moria (?) on the 20^th^. Five
aboriginal children only were at the Mission; but I was informed, they
were gathered at Lake Moira (?), whither I proceeded next morning; but
was unsuccessful in seeing this main body, as they had departed some
eight miles further up; and time did not allow me to follow them up the
river. The school house at Maloga, is, as stated in the application; and
is furnished with a few forms, and three tables. A small stock of slates
and reading books was visible, a Class Roll is kept. It shows, that the
attendance this year, has not exceeded eight pupils, while in 1878,
about 16 attended; but these included some of the young married couples.
In the district, there are 19 children, including two infants, 8 young
married couples, occasionally at school, and 9 aged men and 8 aged
women, parents mostly of the others." Moama 1878

n/d -- (Assume that this page is connected to the one previous and
following) -- So far as I could judge from the five pupils at the
Mission, and from two adults at the Lake I should say, that they can
read the 1^st^ Book fairly, and 2^nd^ book with difficulty. Their
knowledge of arithmetic is slight, Repeating and singing Hymns from a
large portion of their instruction. Of domestic work for the girls, and
farmwork for the boys, little is taught. The prosperity, may, the
existence of the school is dependant upon the ability of Mr. Matthews to
find and clothe the children. And to feed and lodge their friends. Four
hundred pounds was given to the Mission, in 1878 or latter 1877, by the
NSW Government; and this was expended in food and clothing; some 280£,
ad similarly used. While these supplies lasted, the blacks lived
contentedly at the Mission, and children and younger married couples
attended the schools; now, there is no moneys. Therefore no food, they
have gone away, and in my opinion, will not return, until Mr. Matthews
can support them. Very little money has been received in subscriptions
this year; and Mr. Matthews is quite unable to guarantee any sufficient
attendance at the school; all depends, he tells me, upon what help he
receives from friends of the cause."

N/d -- FORM -- Council of Education -- Information to be supplied by
Inspectors when reporting upon Applications for aid to Provisional
Schools. Proposed Provisional School at Maloga.

Where is the proposed school situated?

On the Murray, 14 miles above Moama.

Who are the persons signing the Application as promoters of the School?
State their occupations and respective religious denominations?

Mr Matthews is the only selector: he also is part proprietor of a
Boarding (? Boundry ) Station in Echuca Victoria.

Are they suitable persons to act as a Local Committee? Do you recommend
any others for the office, in addition to, or as substitutes for, the
above mentioned?

Mr Matthews is a respectable man. This is a special case; there are no
other residents except one within several miles of the mission I am
unable therefore to form a Committee.

n/d -- Memorandum of Inspector to the Secretary -- Both Mr. and Mrs.
Matthews are enthusiastic in their devotion to the cause, but the
opinion is held here, that the Mission will never succeed, if managed as
it is now, and from what I saw at Maloga, I am over the same opinion.
Considering, that at present there is practically no school, and that
without active assistance from the Government, or the Public, to provide
food and clothes for the natives at the Mission; there is not likely to
be a school; I would respectfully recommend, that Mr. Matthews be
informed that the Council is unable to assist him, until the school is
reorganized, and is placed on a more permanent basis. With this I
forward specimens of the children's writing taken from their copy book."
