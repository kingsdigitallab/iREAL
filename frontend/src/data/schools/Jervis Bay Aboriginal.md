**Role of the Department and relationship to the APB**

29 June 1932 APB to Dept. Ed. -- "The attached application is forwarded
in accordance with instructions, in connection with an interview Mr. and
Mrs. Roscoe had with the Chief Inspector on the 3^rd^ instant. There is
not at present a vacancy to which Mr. Roscoe could be appointed, but he
could, if considered suitable be kept under notice for nomination to the
first suitable vacancy which occurs." Signed Secretary- Annotation added
"I have personally seen Mr. and Mrs. Roscoe and consider them fitted in
every way for appointment as Teacher Manager and Matron of an Aboriginal
Station. I understand that Mr. Powys is resigning from the Aboriginal
Station at Jervis Bay as from 2^nd^ September next, and in view of Mr.
Roscoe's naval experience and the fact that there are fishing operations
and boats to handle at Jervis Bay, I consider he would be eminently
suitable for appointment to the vacancy."

17 Aug 1932 -- APB to Dept. Ed "Subject: Resignation of Mr. F. G. Powys,
from position of Teacher -- Manager, Aboriginal Station, Jervis Bay;
Nomination of Mr. Rupert Roscoe of 4 Clarke Street Vaucluse to fill
Vacancy -- I beg to forward herewith a communication from Mr. F. G.
Powys, resigning his position as Teacher -- Manager, Aboriginal Station,
Jervis Bay to take effect from 2^nd^. September proximo, which has been
accepted with regret.

At the same time I am directed to nominate Mr. Rupert Roscoe, of 4
Clarke St, Vaucluse, who has already been interviewed by the Deputy
Director of Education, for appointment to fill the vacancy thus created.
Mr. Roscoe's application is attached. Signed Secretary." Annotation
added "I have personally seen Mr. Roscoe and am of the opinion that he
is in every way fitted for the position of teacher of an Aboriginal
School. RECOMMENDED, therefore, that Mr. Roscoe by employed, in terms of
Section 44 of the Public Service Act, 1902, as teacher of the Aboriginal
School at Jervis Bay, with payment at the rate of €5/5/- per week,
subject to the provisions of the Public Service Salaries Act (No.2),
1931 -- 32

**Teachers**

Memo from Teacher to Dept Ed. 7 June 1932 -- "I beg to report that
during the last week of the Autumn vacation, I visited Sydney mainly on
work connected with the Aboriginal Station. Owing to car trouble delay
was caused, and I did not resume duty until 1^st^ June, one day after
the opening of schools. It is my intention to make up the time lost,
during the following week." Teacher in charge -- F.G Powys

Teacher F G Powys -- tendering resignation on 4 August 1932 to the
Secretary of the Aborigines Protection Board Sydney "It is with regret
that I tender my resignation as Manager of Jervis Bay Aboriginal
Station, same to take effect from 2^nd^ September. An offer has been
made to me from Sydney, and after due consideration, I have decided to
accept."

7 September 1932 -- Dept. Ed noting approval by the Public Service Board
for the employment of Mr. R. Roscoe to the position of Teacher
Aboriginal School Jervis Bay in terms of Section 44 of the Public
Service Act 1902, with pay at the rate of €4.2.6 p.w

> *Experience*

Application from Mrs. Roscoe to APB -- "Dear Sir, With reference to my
husband's application for a position in your department, I would like to
add that I have had experience in handling natives in New Guinea, and
understand the treatment of simple ailments peculiar to them. I have
also had experience as a pupil teacher in South Australia, teaching all
Primary subjects and Elementary music."
