**Role of the Department and relationship to the APB**

23/4/1915 -- APB -- Police Department, Inspector Generals Office, Sydney
to Under Secretary Dept. Public Instruction "Sir, I am directed by my
Board to ask whether £50 per annum is the maximum amount which your
Department is prepared to pay the Teacher (Miss Dietrich) of the
Aborigines School at Mount Olive, via Singleton." Note from Dept. "This
is a disagreeable position to hold and it is not easy to get the right
person. I think a special salary of £80 might be given provided that
full time is taught. If an average attendance of 10 would be kept up for
the minimum salary of £1 would be payable, but this is rather doubtful.
I recommend that proposal be submitted to the P. S. Board."

5/4/1916 -- Mount Olive (Subsidised aboriginal school), near Singleton
-- Proposed conversion to Provisional rank "It has been decided that the
above-named school shall rank as provisional from the date of entry on
duty of Mr. T. H. Austin, who will succeed Miss. Dietrich, the present
teacher, at an early date."

18/4/1916 -- Dept. Education memo re Mount Olive (Aborigines'
Subisidised School): - Proposed conversion to Provisional rank. "The
above-named aborigines' school has been conducted as a subsidized
school, the teacher being paid a special salary of £80 per annum. There
are sufficient children enrolled to justify its conversion to
Provisional rank, and it is proposed to appoint an aborigines' school
teacher upon the resignation of Miss Dietrich, at present in charge.
Miss Dietrich's resignation will take place shortly, and her successor
will take up the duties at the usual rate of salary paid to teachers of
aborigines' schools (£110 per annum). I recommend that the school be
converted to Provisional rank from the date of the new teacher's entry
on duty."

25/4/1916 -- APB to Dept. Education -- "Sir, I beg to ask that Mr. T. H.
Austin's appointment to the Aborigines School at Mount Olive, near
Singleton, be allowed to stand over for about 6 or 7 weeks, in order to
permit of his taking service with the Board as Relieving Manager during
that time. His allowance as Teacher to of course take effect from the
date he takes up duty at Mount Olive."

5/9/1916 -- Memo to Dept. Education -- re closing of school and
Singleton show. "Sir, I have the honor to inform you that nearly all the
residents on this Station and their families have gone to Singleton for
the show which last three days. This means that no children at School
until Monday next (11^th^ September'16). I would suggest that September
7^th^ and 8^th^ be counted as holidays, and that the school be opened
for two days of the M (?) holidays. This show seems to be the Dark
peoples annual holiday and they never miss attending." Note to Chief
Inspector "I recommend that the teacher be authorized to open his school
for two days in the Michaelmas (?) Vacation (as he suggests) in place of
the two days that his school is closed for the show - not including the
day to which authority has been given for closing."

8/6/1920 -- APB to Dept. Education re Transfer of MR. T. H. Austin from
the position of Teacher-Manager, Mount Olive Station, to the position of
Teacher-Superintendent, Aborigines Home, Singleton -- "I am directed to
inform you that at the last meeting of the Board, it was decided to
dispense with the services of Mr. G. C. Smith, Superintendent of the
Aborigines Home at Singleton, and to recommend the transfer of Mr. T. H.
Austin to the dual position of Teacher-Superintendent at the Home. As
the matter was urgent Mr. Austin has been instructed, in anticipation of
your concurrence, to meet the Board's Inspector at Singleton for the
purpose of taking over control at the Home. I have instructed him to
advise you of the date he relinquishes duty at Mt Olive and takes up
duty at Singleton. The question of filling the vacancy at Mount Olive is
receiving consideration, and will be made the subject of later
communication." Signed A.C. Pettit.

19/1/1923 -- APB to Dept. Education re Aborigines School, Mount Olive --
Resignation of Teacher and Suggested Closure of School -- " I beg to
advise that Miss. A. C. Hayter, teacher of the Aborigines School at
Mount Olive, near Singleton, has been appointed to a position under my
Board at the Girls Training Home, Cootamundra, where she will commence
duty on the 1^st^ proximo. Her resignation is attached accordingly, and
it is requested that a notation be made on your returns to the
Superannuation Board to the effect that she has been transferred to the
employ of this Department. Regarding the vacancy created at Mount Olive
it is suggested that steps be not taken to have it filled. I might state
that I visited the Reserve a few weeks ago and then ascertained that
there were only three Aboriginal children and two white children
attending, and subsequent inquiries disclosed that about four
prospective pupils will not now be available. Very few Aborigines now
use the Reserve and it is thought that the school may with safety be
permanently closed. In anticipation of your concurrence, therefore, the
Superintendent of the Boys' Home Singleton, has been instructed to take
charge of the Mount Olive School material after the 31^st^ instant, and
transfer same to the Home for use there." Signed A.C. Pettit.

**Teachers**

16/1/1915 -- Letter from Miss. Dietrich to Senior Constable Hanney "Dear
Sir, I am very sorry to have to inform you that Miss. Dodimean is at
present seriously ill in the Dangar Cottage Hospital. Dr. Parry, who is
attending her, says it will likely be several weeks before she could
return to Mt Olive. He cannot, as yet, state the cause of her illness,
but suspects Typhoid Fever. Therefore, the question arises as to who
will take charge of the Mt Olive Aborigines School during her absence.
For several months Miss. Dodimeads health has been failing, and I have
had to assist her in her duties as Teacher. To do this, I had to gain
the permission of the Local Board here at Mt Olive. They readily gave
their consent. The Teachers who have acted for the Inspector in the last
2 yrs inspection also raised no objection. I therefore make an
application for the position of Teacher of the Mt Olive school during
her absence and until such times as she is able to resume duty again. As
I am well acquainted with the children and the working of the school, I
have no fear in asking may keep her place open for her. I might state
that some time ago I wrote to the Sydney A.P. B. applying for the
position as teacher to any Aboriginal School where there was a vacancy.
The reply I received from Mr. Beardsmore was that such a vacancy occur
he would let me know. I sent with the application several testimonials,
which have not been returned. Will you kindly report Miss. Dodimeads
illness to the Head Board and inform me of the result as early as
possible."

18/1/1915 -- Police Station Singleton -- Subject -- Re illness of Miss
Dodimead, Teacher of Mount Olive subsidized School. "Senior Constable
Hanney begs to report receiving the attached letter from Miss. E.
Dietrich, concerning the ill health of the teacher above referred to,
Dr. Parry of Singleton informed the Senior Constable that it would be
some time before Miss. Dodimead would be able to resume her duties, as
everything points to her having an attack of Typhoid Fever. Miss.
Dietrich has been a companion to Miss Dodimead for some time at the St
Clair Reserve, helping to teach the Aborigines, with the approval (she
says) of the Local Aborigine Board, Under the circumstances perhaps she
could be appointed temporarily to teach the children, until Miss.
Dodimead has recovered from her illness."

28/5/1915 -- Mount Olive Subsidised School: Question of payment to
Teacher. "In connection with the illness and subsequent death of Miss
Dodimead, Miss E. Dietrich was recognized by this Department on 26^th^.
February last, as Teacher of this Subsidised School. She was later
(20^th^. April) informed that in view of the fact that the pupils only
received instruction for one half of the day, subsidy at £3 per head, up
to a maximum of £30 per annum would only be allowed. Against this the
Teacher protested, pointing out that the half time arrangement existed
under the previous teacher who was missionary as well as teacher, but
that she, Miss. Dietrich,w as able to give her whole time to the
teaching, and from the 1^st^ April started "all day" school. It was
impossible to live on £30 per annum allowance. The Secretary,
Aborigines' Protection Board, has also written, inquiring whether £50
p.a. is the maximum amount which this Department is prepared to pay the
Teacher. The Principal Senior Inspector recommends, that owing to the
difficulties of the position, it be submitted to the Public Service
Board that a special salary of £80 might be given, provided that full
time be taught. The time this should date from should be fixed. This,
although a special case, is outside the Regulations for subsidized
schools. Perhaps it might be regarded as a Provisional School for
Aborigines, and the Teacher appointed at the salary stated, with no
claim to any other position under this Department. Thirteen children
appear in the schedule, and three others since enrolled (one 5 years
old). Miss Dietrich also draws attention to a deduction of 3/10 from
March subsidy, on which report might be obtained)≥

16/6/1915 -- Letter to Teacher Miss. E. Dietrich. Subsidised School,
Mount Olive, via Singleton Re Your Letter of 28^th^. April last,
pointing out that the subsidy as present paid in connection with the
school, viz. , £3 per child per annum, is insufficient, and stating that
you are able to give your whole time to the teaching. "With reference to
the above, I have to inform you that, as a special case, the Public
Service Board have approved of you being employed as a full time teacher
of the Mount Olive Subsidised School, with pay at the rate of £80 per
annum from 1^st^. June, 1915, on the understanding that you have no
claim thereby to any other position under this Department."

26/4/1915 -- Letter from Teacher Miss. Dietrich to Dept. Education re
question of salary Note at end of letter "PS: Sir, Will you kindly
forward me some envelopes please, and also a form for the enrolment of
new pupils, I wish to enroll the names of Timothy Lester aged 6 years of
Speaks Flat and Jack Lester (aged 6) of Rock Station. I have placed the
name of Jean Miller aged 4 years 11 months on the roll. When I did so I
did not know that permission had to be granted for same. Shall I include
her in the request."

6/4/1915 -- Letter from Teacher Miss. Dietrich to Dept. Education re
question of salary

20/5/1915 -- Copy of Letter sent from Dept. Public Instruction to Mr. F.
J. Boyce Mount Olive "Sir, With reference to my letter of 11^th^.
Ultimo, intimating that the Minister of Public Instruction had approved
of the recognition of Miss Dietrich as Teacher of the Subsidised School
at Mount Olive, and also that subsidy should not exceed the sum of £50
per annum, I have to inform you that, in view of the fact that the
children attending the school only receive instruction for one half of
the day, subsidy at the rate of £ per pupil per annum on the average
monthly attendance up to a maximum of £30 per annum can only be
allowed." Signed Under Secretary

19/1/1923 -- Letter from Teacher A. C. Hayter Aborigines School, Mount
Olive to Dept. Education -- "Dear Sir, Having been appointed to a
position under the Aborigines Protection Board I beg to tender my
resignation as Teacher of the above school, and to request that my
Superannuation contributions be transferred to the Aborigines Protection
Board. I desire that this my resignation date from the 31^st^ instant."

> *Experience*

Miss. Dietrich application for position as Teacher -- "I am 24 years of
age, and was educated at Swanston Street S.S. Geelong and Flinders
School Geelong, Victoria. For subjects such as Cookery, Singing, (?) etc
I attended the Gordon Technical College Geelong. I assisted the late
Miss Dodimead in her duties as Teacher in the Subsidised School at Mt
Olive. I will be prepared to take up duties on 1^st^ March 1915. I have
been assisting since July 9^th^ 1913 at Mt Olive. Miss Dodimead sent a
testimonial to the Sydney Aboriginal Protection Board. I do not have a
copy. Application includes a list of children in attendance at the
School -- Ages of Children and parents signature. Families include
Waters, Davies, Miller, Samuells, Walsh, Lester and Smith.

12/6/1920 -- Aborigines Home Singleton -- to Chief Inspector of Schools,
Education Buildings Sydney "Dear Sir, I have to acknowledge receipt of
your telegram instructing me to take charge of the Aborigines School
Singleton as from 28^th^ June. By the same mail I received notice from
the Sec. Aborigines Board that I was to return to Mt Olive immediately
to Superintendt was appointed. As you are a member of the Board I
respectfully desire to put the facts of my case before you. I asked the
Secr. of the Board to give us an assurance that proper accommodation
would be provided to me and my wife and family at once and I obtained
contracts for what I considered a fair provision. The Board inspector
Mr. Swindlehurst left me on Thursday having transferred dockets to me.
He gave me no advise except that I was to take charge and occupy present
quarters consisting of two rooms. I may state that the place is
verminous and I should be doing a great injustice if I brought my wife
and three little children into such a place, and I would not do it. Mr.
Inspector Buck, knows what kind of residence we have at Mt Olive and
this place is worse. I am quite certain if the member of the Board saw
the place they would not allow us to occupy it. I asked Mr.
Superintendent what would happen if I refused to occupy the position; he
said that very likely I should be dismissed. In view of these
circumstances will your telegram still hold good could take the school
and have nothing to do with the management of the home. If this
arrangement can be made I can easily get a house. We have always worked
hard both for the Board and Education Department and this
misunderstanding has very much upset me. I have as the Board Secretary
to give me an opportunity of stating my case to the Board." Signed Mr.
Thomas. H. Austin

31/7/1916 -- Memo to Dept. Education from Thomas Austin Teacher, Manager
re Outbreak of Diphtheria as advised by telegram 21^st^ July 1916. "Sir,
I have the honor to send to you a report respecting outbreak of
Diphtheria in this school as advised in my telegram of today. Timothy
Lester aged 8 is seriously ill, and his brother Ted Lester, aged six, is
slightly affected. Two other scholars are absent through sickness and I
am making inquiries respecting nature of sickness. I would recommend
that this school have the Mic(?) vacation forthwith as this disease is
likely to spread amongst this class of children. This slight vacation
would give an opportunity of noting either "carriers" or affected
cases." Note from Dept. Education There does not at present appear any
reason why this school should be closed more especially as so many days
have passed without any further trouble occurring. The teacher might be
advised to carry out the requirements of circular 6 amended reissue."

5/8/1916 -- Dept. Education Memo to Teacher Mr. T. H. Austin re Your
telegram of the 1^st^ instant reporting the outbreak of Diphtheria in
your locality. "I have to inform you that, from the other information
supplied by you in the above, there does not appear any need to close
your school. You should furnish hereunder a full report and return this
memorandum direct to this office without delay." Note from teacher
"There are two cases of diphtheria from this school, both children
residing off the station. They are fairly serious too. Two other
children were away sick all last week but one returned today. There are
no other children showing any symptoms and I have burnt plenty of
sulphur in the school room. I suggested leaving (?) holidays so that any
possible inspection might be avoided. I do not think however that it
would (?) be necessary to take this step as it is over a week since the
cases were reported."

11/8/1916 -- Dept. Education Memo to Mr. T. H. Austin Teacher of the
Aboriginal Provisional School at Mount Olive, Singleton. Re "Your report
of the 31^st^ July on the outbreak of diphtheria in the locality. --
With reference to the above I have to inform you that there does not at
present appear any reason why your school should be closed, more
especially as so many days have now passed without any further trouble
occurring. You should however carry out the requirements of Circular No.
6 (amended re-issue), and state hereunder the period for which your
school has been closed. This memorandum should be returned directed to
this office without delay." Note from teacher "The school was not closed
at all I waited until I received instructions before taking action in
this direction. I have not yet received any notice of the outbreak from
the Local Health Authority. My information was the result of a personal
inquiry at the Local Hospital" Signed Thomas, H. Austin Teacher, Manager
