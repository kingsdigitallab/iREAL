17/10/1933 -- Menindee, Carowra Tank, Pooncarie: Education for
Aboriginal children "The Board for the Protection of Aborigines has
closed the Aboriginal schools at Carowra Tank and Pooncarie and
transferred the families to a new site about six miles north of the town
of Menindee. The Board asks that approval be given for the transfer of
the teachers (Mr. C.A Murkins, Pooncarie and Mr. J. G. Park Carowra Tank
to Menindee. This has been done. IT IS RECOMMENDED that formal approval
be given for

the closing of the Aboriginal Provisional Schools at Carowra Tank and
Pooncarie

The establishment of an Aboriginal Provisional School at Menindee and

Transfer of Messrs Murkins and Park to Menindee"

Dept Education -- Menindee, Carowra Tank and Pooncarie: The Aboriginal
Provisional Schools at Carowra Tank and Pooncarie have been closed. A
new school of this class has been established at Menindee (Broken Hill
District)."

APB -- 6/10/1933 -- "Subject: The removal of Aborigines from Carowra
Tank and Pooncarie New Station at Menindee -- I beg to advise that owing
to the failure of the water supply at Carowra Tank, it became necessary
to close the station, and transfer it to a new site on the river, six
miles north of the town of Menindee. The transfer was completed on the
29^th^. Ultimo. It is also intended to transfer the Aborigines now
located at Pooncarie to the new station at Menindee, and to remove the
school building from the Pooncarie reserve to the new site. This is
being done immediately as it is urgently required, and I am directed to
ask that approval be given for the transfer of the teacher, Mr. C. A
Murkins, to Menindee, as assistant to the teacher-Manager, Mr. J. G.
Park. His immediate presence at Menindee would be a wonderful assistance
to Mr. Park, who now has a tremendous lot to attend to, not only in the
ordinary administration of the station, but also the erection of
buildings, and the carrying out of improvements. In the event of your
Department approving Mr. Murkins transfer my Board has sanctioned his
appointment as Teacher-Manager. I might state that taking into account
the numbers from Carowra Tank, Pooncarie, and several who were
previously residing at Menindee, there will be nearly 90 children of
school age available to attend the school."

26/6/1939 -- Department of Education -- Menindee Aboriginal School:
Termination of services of Mr. Thomas Arthur Blackshaw, Assistant
Teacher. "At a meeting of the Board for the Protection of Aborigines
held on Wednesday, 14^th^ June, 1939, reports in connection with the
case of Mr. Thomas Arthur Blackshaw, Assistant Teacher-Manager,
Aboriginal Station, Menindee, were considered. An allegation had been
made through Mr. Michael Sawtell, President of the Committee for
Aboriginal Citizenship, and also Mr. J. G. Danvers, former Manager of
the station, that Mr. Blackshaw was the father of an illegitimate child
born to a half-caste girl named Flora Smith. The papers had been
referred to the Child Welfare with a view o the institution of
proceedings for maintenance, but that Department considered that the
information furnished is not sufficient to provide corroboration
necessary for affiliation proceedings. The Board for the Protection of
Aborigines decided that, in view of Mr. Blackshaw's conduct and
indiscreet relations with the half-caste girl, Flora Smith, which could
not fail to have a subversive effect on the people on the Station and on
his position as a teacher, the papers be referred to this Department
with a view to the termination of his employment. RECOMMENDED that Mr.
Thomas Arthur Blackshaw's services as a temporary Assistant Teacher
(Section 44) under this Department be terminated -- last day of service
being Friday 30^th^ June, 1939."

20/4/1939 -- Termination of services of Mr. and Mrs. J. G. Danvers,
Teacher- Manager and Matron, Aborigines Station, Menindee. "In am
directed by the Chief Secretary to intimate that, following on
representations made to him in relation to the abovementioned matter,
arrangements were made for the Public Service Board to hold an
investigation. This action has been taken and the Board have now
furnished a report, intimating their view: -

"a) Mr. Danvers failed to carry out efficiently the duties attaching to
his position as Teacher- Manager at Menindee.;

b\) Mrs. Danvers, while carrying out satisfactorily within the limits of
her capacity the duties of Matron, was not qualified to meet the demands
necessarily made on the Matron at that Station;

c\) The Aborigines Protection Board has no alternative but to terminate
the employment of Mr. and Mrs. Danvers as Manager and Matron at
Menindee\\;

d\) No other position for which Mr. Danvers would be suitable was
available at the date of the termination of his services, even had his
administration of Menindee been such as to warrant a continuation of his
services;

e\) as teaching duties form an important part of the duties of Manager,
it was inevitable that Mr. Danvers attitude to those duties must have
led ultimately to his services being reviewed, even had he remained at
Kinchela and not been transferred to Menindee." Signed Under Secretary.

8/3/1938 -- Memo from Mr. Danvers to Chief Inspector of Schools --
Misunderstanding regarding the position of Superintendent of Training
Home. "Dear Sir, I attach a copy of W. Inspector Bruce's instructions to
me and feel that there is a misunderstanding of the position. At the
time of my appointment by the Aborigines Protection Board as
Superintendent of the Aborigines Boys Training Home, Kinchela. My wife
and self were interviewed by you at your office. I then said that I had
no experience in or qualifications for school teaching. I understood
from you that when an assistant teacher was appointed my duties would be
to take off the senior boys and train them in outdoor occupations that
would assist them in their life when placed in employment from here.
This training of boys in outdoor work appears to be a very essential
part of the work of this Home and my thirty years experience on the land
enables me to give them that training ...."

4/3/1938 -- Annual School Inspection -- Inspector G. H. Bruce. "Mr.
Danvers, Since the advent of the assistant teacher has evidently
misunderstood his position with relation to the school. He has been
giving instructions in manual training and such other activities as are
conducted outside the school proper. From now on he should run the
school as a two teacher school distributing the teaching load equitably
between himself and the assistant. In order that this organization may
be effective the school should be divided into two divisions, an upper
and a lower, each teacher should take charge of a division. Please
arrange to have this change made immediately and advise me as soon as
the new organization is in operation."

Letter to Mr. Danvers from Chief Inspector 28/3/1938 -- Re
Misunderstanding regarding your position at Kinchela. "There has
certainly been a misunderstanding on your part concerning your functions
as teacher. In our interviews last year, I stressed the point that
during the time programmed for manual training you could, owing to your
special qualifications, give to the senior boys work of such a practical
kind as would fit them for their future vocations. For the rest, you
were to teach formal subjects. You are referred to my circular forwarded
to you recently concerning the teaching duties of Teacher-Managers at
Aboriginal Stations. The contents of this memorandum apply as fully to
you as to other officials in similar capacities. I was disappointed to
read Mr. District Inspector Bruce's recent report on your functions as a
teacher. As a result of this report your mark for the present has been
deferred. Later in the year a further report on your efficiency as a
teacher will be obtained." Not ed on bottom of Memo -- to Chief
Inspector Bruce -- For your information. Would you please keep in touch
with this school and arrange for a second inspection later in the year?

18/9/1937 -- Memo to APB concerning claim for Workers Compensation --
Mr. J. G. Park, Teacher, Aboriginal Station, Menindee. -- Cause loss of
a middle finger, joint of index finger and 1^st^ and 2^nd^ joints of
ring finger.

"On 30^th^ April, 1937, I was engaged, in company with Jack Williams and
Martin Webster, in baling water from the pump shaft at the Aboriginal
Station, Menindee. When we had lowered the level of the water below the
driving belt of the pump, the Manager, Mr. Park. Instructed Williams,
who was working below, to come up as he was going to start the engine.
After he had started the engine he himself went down the shaft to see
that everything was working satisfactorily. I remained with the others
outside the engine house. In about five minutes I saw Mr. Park come out
of the shaft with his left hand covered with blood and apparently, badly
mangled. We assisted him up to the house, and left him in charge of his
wife. Signed Herbert Johnson."

Claim sent on to the APB for compensation and is under consideration by
the Government Insurance Office.

5/11/1937 -- Claim for Workers Compensation -- Mr. J. G. Park,

*Students -- conditions of school building*

School Repairs -- Inspection of School - 11/6/1936 -- building was
unceiled -- although ceiling material was still lying in the room. "This
work is most necessary owing to the great heat in this locality during
the summer months. The class rooms are unbearable and little work of
value can be done. In addition, a verandah is required on the western
side of the building to protect it, and top give some shelter to the
children. The W. C's are in an unsatisfactory condition. The approaches
have been washed away, the floors of the interior need repairs and a
proper boys' urinal needs erection. In view of the urgent necessity of
the works outlined above, it is RECOMMENDED that the A.P Board be asked
to (1) Ceil the school with the material at hand. (2) Have a verandah
erected on the western side of the building and (3) make repairs to the
W. C's."
