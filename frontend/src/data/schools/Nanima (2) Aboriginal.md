6/1/1908 -- Aborigines Mission Camp Reserve, Wellington -- Application
for establishment of school. Following details are included in
application:

> 3 miles to post office
>
> Total of 40 students (27 boys and 12 girls)
>
> 15 Church of England, 24 Roman Catholic and 1 Presbyterian.
>
> No School Building
>
> Land -- Aboriginal Reserve

Includes a list of children and parents.

> What is the population of the locality with a radius of two miles of
> the site of the proposed school? -- "Aborigines, About 14 families
> consisting from 80 to 90 persons. There are between 50 & 60 children,
> of whom 36 are school age."
>
> Is the locality likely to be permanently inhabited? What are the
> occupations of the residents? "Yes, the families are settled on a
> reserve set apart for Aborigines. They have practically no
> occupation." "I think the reserve will be permanently occupied, but
> the wandering instincts of the race must always be borne in mind."
>
> A suitable site can be obtained on the reserve, 5 chs by 4 chs,
> central, east of access and quite suitable, and will doubtless by
> given unconditionally by the Aborigines Board.
>
> Distances of homes from the school? -- The families are living side by
> side on the reserve, within a stones throw of the proposed site. No
> local physical features would interfere with.
>
> Who are the promoters of the proposed school? -- Rev A Stephen,
> Wesleyan Minister and Miss I Hetherington, Aborigines Missionary
>
> Any other information? -- "It is, I consider, advisable to educate
> these children, in a School of their own, and so to keep them separate
> from the white children."
>
> Signed J Wright, Inspector -- Orange 22/2/1908

Recommended that the school be established.

25/2/1908 -- Nanima: Summary report on Application for the Establishment
of a Public School for Aborigines and Recommendation. "There is a
reserve for Aborigines on the Macquarie River, about 3 miles east of the
town of Wellington. About 14 families have collected there, and a
missionary has been appointed to take charge of the settlement. There
are from 50 to 60 children, mostly half-castes, and of these 36 are at
present of school age. A few of these children attend the Wellington
School, but not very regularly, and many of the white inhabitants of the
town object strongly to their presence. I consider it very advisable to
give them a school of their own, and so recommend: - (1) That a Public
School for Aborigines be established at Nanima. (2) That the Aborigines
Protection Board be asked to grant a site of 2 acres (3) That a
building, similar to that to be erected at Goolma, be built under
inspectorial supervision. " J Wright Inspector. Note "Refer to AP Board.
I think it might be considered by the Board whether the school buildings
required for Aborigines should not be erected by the Board.

3/3/1908 -- APB "Refer to Mr. Bell for report by the Wellington Police.
A plan and specification for a suitable school room might be prepared
and an estimate of cost submitted. All that is required is an
inexpensive, comfortable building."

9/4/1908 -- Dept. Public Instruction to APB -- "Sir, referring to the
formal application for the establishment of a Public School for
Aborigines at Nanima (Aborigines Mission Camp Reserve, Wellington) I
have the honor to inform you that, having a considered report upon the
matter, the Minister of Public Instruction has resolved to establish a
Public School at the place in question, on condition that the requisite
school building is erected by the Aborigines Protection Board, in
accordance with the enclosed plan, at an estimated cost of Ninety pounds
(£90) to be charged to this Department. Papers relating to the building
and specification are returned herewith."

22/4/1908 -- APB to Mr. J. Bell, Superintendent of Police Bathurst --
"Referred to Mr. Bell for information and favour of necessary action.
Please obtain tenders locally for the erection of a building in
accordance with the attached plan, and favour the Board with a
recommendation."

6/9/1910 -- Letter from Teacher Ardill to Dept. Public Instruction "Sir,
I have the honour to ask that the unavoidable cessation of my work
caused by the removal, by the Aborigines Protection Board, of the above
school to the New Reserve, be sanctioned. The school was pulled down, by
order of the AP Board, on 15^th^ August, and rebuilt, lined, ceiled and
a fire place added. It has therefore been closed from 15^th^ August to 5
September inclusive, and was reopened by me today." Note to Chief
Inspector "The Aborigines have been shifted to a reserve about 2 miles
farther from Wellington. Miss Ardill did not know when the school was to
be moved till the workmen arrived to start the job, so she could not
apply in advance for the closing of the school."

6/9/1910 -- Teacher to Dept. Public Instruction "Sir, I have the honour
to state that I am often asked to allow this school to be used as a
concert-room, a dancing hall, or a sleeping apartment for visitors. I
have refused to do so, but wish to know exactly what is permissible. The
school is used every Sunday, two services being held here, as well as
Sunday School. There is also occasionally a prayer-meeting on Wednesday
morning. This has been allowed, because there is no other building
available for such meetings."

7/9/1910 -- APB to Dept. Public Instruction "Sir, I beg to acknowledge
receipt of your letter of the 5^th^ instant, no.10/8444, in regard to
the removal of the school building for Aborigines at Nanima
(Wellington), and regret that your Department was not officially advised
of the proposed removal. Should any cases of the kind occur in future
your Department will be duly informed, and I shall be glad if you will
reciprocate by notifying the Board when any School for Aborigines is
closed (temporarily or otherwise) by your Department."

9/10/1910 -- Police Station Wellington -- Subject: - Use of school room
at Mission Station, Wellington. Sunday service concerts and dancing hall
. Sergeant (?) reports that he visited the Mission this day and
interviewed Miss Ardill also Miss Hetherington Teacher and Missionary.
Both state that for the past three weeks the school has not been used
for any of the purposes above stated. But previously it had been. Miss
Hetherington states that she has no desire to ask for its use again but
intends asking for a church to be erected for the use of Aborigines on
the mission station. No spilling is now done on the floor and the school
building is clean." Forwarded to APB

12/9/1910 -- Memo to the Chief Inspector "I recommend that the school
building not be granted for the Church services, Prayer meetings, or
other meetings. My reasons are (1) That some of the Aborigines are
suffering from phthisis and they expectorate freely on the floor. This
must be a menace to the health of the children and teacher. (2) That,
after these meetings, the room is left in a very dirty condition, and
extra expense and work and thereby caused."

9/6/1926 -- Letter from Teacher to Inspector of Schools re increase of
attendance. "Sir, Allow me to draw your attention to the increase in
enrolment of pupils since your inspection on May 28^th^ at Aboriginal
School Wellington. Four new pupils enrolled bringing number to nine.
There are five children under 5 yrs living on reserve and two of these
children will be 5 yrs in October. Bringing the number to eleven. I
heard yesterday of two families, living in town receiving notice to
quit. So they will be looking for houses, and very likely will return to
mission.

6/5/1927 -- Letter from Teacher to Inspector of Schools re closing of
school for two days for the Wellington show. Mr. Dunn the Minister for
Agriculture also visited the school.

28/5/1928 -- Letter from Teacher to Inspector of Schools. "Dear Sir,
Will you kindly advise me on the following matter. There has been a
quarrel on the Mission between two women. One is summoning for assault
and the other is cross summoning for insulting words. I received a
subpoena to attend the court case next Friday May 28^th^. Will the
Department stop from my pay the time lost in going to court. And should
I claim a days pay from losing party. The case may take any time
(perhaps a few minutes) I hold position of Matron as well as Teacher on
the Mission. The school is 3 1/2 miles from the town."

31/5/1928 -- Memo to Miss. W. Lake Teacher of the Abor. Prov. School at
Nanima Wellington re Court case from Dept. Education. Please furnish
particulars of any expenses received as a witness under the Subpoena
together with any expenses received from parties' calling for your
evidence. Note from Teacher "Dear Sir, No expenses have been received by
me to date. The cases were postponed from May 28^th^ to June 22^nd^.
Further particulars will be forwarded in due course."

6/6/1928 -- Letter from Teacher Lake to Inspector of Schools re court
case "Sir, I beg to inform you that the Court Cases set down for 22^nd^
June were settled out of Court. The parties did not appear. I did not
receive any expenses and went to school on Friday as usual."

11/4/1930 -- Memo from Teacher to Dept. re permission to close school to
attend Annual Show.

15/4/1935 -- Memo from Teacher re Fuel Allowance "Sir, During the past
two years I have not had any allowance for fuel. It was with difficulty
of personal cost to myself that we had fires in the school, may I please
have an allowance granted for this coming winter. I have 34 pupils
enrolled. On account of a number of children not being suitably clothed.
I had a fire lighted this morning. Trusting you will grant me this
request." R. Taylor -- Note to Chief Secretary "It is not the practice
of the Department to grant fuel allowance to Aboriginal Schools. The
teacher should seek assistance from the Protection Board. Recommended
that he be informed accordingly." Approved.

3/12/1936 -- APB to Dept. Education Subject: Retirement of Miss. Rose
Taylor from Position of Teacher, Aborigines School, Nanima Via
Wellington -- Question of replacement "I beg to advise that Miss. Rose
Taylor, Teacher, Aborigines School, Nanima, via Wellington, has
intimated that she will be retiring from that position at the end of the
present School term. It is therefore recommended that the vacancy thus
created be filled by the transfer of Miss Mabel F. Fraser, at present
Teacher of the Aboriginal School at Burnt Bridge via Kempsey, who has
signified her willingness to this arrangement. The matter of Burnt
Bridge might be allowed to stand in abeyance, in view of a proposal
under the Board' s consideration at the present time, that the
Aboriginal Station at Urunga be closed, and the residents transferred to
Kempsey where a Station would be established under present
Teacher-Manager of Urunga, Mr. J. J. Jacobs."

10/6/1937 -- Teacher Fraser writing to Dept. Education re fuel allowance
as fires have been lit at school on cold days. Reply from Department to
Teacher "I refer to your memorandum of the 30^th^ Ultimo, addressed to
the Accountant of this Department, in which you indicate that a sum of
5/- has been expended on the purchase of fuel for use at the school
under your charge. In this connection I would refer you to my
communication of 25^th^ ultimo, wherein you were informed that this
Department does not provide an allowance towards the cost of purchasing
fuel at Aborigines' Schools and that this is a matter for the Aborigines
Protection Board."

6/4/1938 -- Letter from Teacher at Nanima to Dept. Education re Salary
rate "Dear Sir, would you be kind enough to let me know, what my salary
is now according to the Education. It does not correspond. Under the nil
classification of 12 years my salary is not to that amount. I've been in
the Department teacher over 21(?) years. In an Aboriginal School make
the difference. Will you please let me know if it is not much trouble
soon. My last salary was £7-12-7." Signed Miss Fraser. Note from Chief
Inspector "Inform Miss Fraser that as a teacher of an Aborigines'
school, she is not entitled to increased salary in terms of the teachers
award."

Reply to Miss. Fraser from Department Education 6/5/1938 Subject: -
Salary Inquiry-- "In reply to your communication of the 6^th^ ultimo
respecting the above mentioned subject, I wish to inform you that, as a
teacher of an Aborigines' School, you are not entitled to increase
salary in terms of the Teachers' Award." Signed B.C. Harkness Chief
Inspector.

*Experience*

1/4/1912 -- Teacher Ardill making enquiries about superannuation. "Sir,
I have the honour to inquire if the money (4% of my salary) paid by me
towards the Teachers' Superannuation Fund, from January 1887 to June
1892 inclusive can be returned to me. I was assistant teacher at Adelong
Public School during that period. I had a III B Certificate, and
received £114per annum. I resigned in June 1892, in order to establish
and take charge of a High School at Rarotonga, Cook Islands, for native
boys belonging to the Cook group. After five years of strenuous work my
health failed, and I was compelled to leave the Tropics, and the service
of the London Missionary Society. I afterwards joined the Melanesian
Mission and was senior Teacher of girls at the M. M. College, Norfolk
Island, from February 1900 till May 1909, when I returned to New South
Wales. I was appointed to the Aborigines' School in which I now teacher,
in July 1909, and received £84 per annum till January 1911, when my
salary was raised to £100 per annum. As I received only £34 per annum at
Norfolk Island I was unable to save any money while there; and I have
not saved much here, either, having had to spend between £40 and £50 on
furniture since I came. (As it was necessary for me to live near the
school, and I could not procure board and lodging nearer than Wellington
(5 miles distant) the AP Board erected a two-roomed cottage which I had
to furnish). My desire is to buy a piece of land and have a home built
on it before I have to retire, so that I may be able to support myself
by bee-keeping or poultry farming, when I can no longer do so by
teaching; and if the money paid in by me towards the Superannuation fund
could be refunded, it would be of great service to me."

**Students**

5/10/1914 -- Aborigines Reserve Wellington Letter from Maggie May, Aged
12 years to Secretary APB -- "Dear Sir, The Teacher asked me to write to
you and tell you about the prizes we who come to this school have won.
Second Prize in the N.S.W League of Bird Lovers yearly competition for
an essay about a "Willy Wagtail". This year I wont he League's First
Prizes for an essay on the "Autobiography of an Australian Wild Bird",
and the prize is 10/6 d. I also won second prize at the Wellington
Annual Show this year for best crayon drawing. I drew a dish of fruit.
The dish was green with white flannel flowers on it, and I put a pear, a
plum, an orange, and a bunch each of green and purple grapes in the
dish. My brother who is ten years of age, won at the same show the First
Prize for an original story called "The eagle and the crow". This
competition was open to all school children in the Wellington District,
and the prize was a gold medal. Also two of our school boys named Daley
and James Stanley won both First prize and second prize at the same show
for an essay called "The story of a grain of wheat". Both the boys are
twelve years old. We girls would have put some sewing in the show, but
made dressed this year to wear to the show, so could not exhibit them. I
remain, Your little friend (Sgd) Maggie May Aged 12 years"
