**Role of the Department and relationship to the APB**

20/8/1892 -- Letter re Wauchope (Aboriginal) Requesting use of building
for religious instruction -- "Mr. Baker Dear Sir, I now take the
pleasure of writing you these few lines hoping to find you in good
health as it haves me at present. I was down at Pelican Island about 3
weeks ago and I found the people getting on well down there in regards
of keeping away from drink and they seem very anxious for myself a great
improvement amongst them since hey have heard the Gospel and they are
also getting on remarkable at Wauchope and the school at Wauchope is
finished today and pleased let me know weather I can have it on Sundays
to hold religious services on. I remain truly Signed Robert Field."
Additional note "I would beg to recommend that permission be asked from
the Department of Education to allow religious services to be held on
Sundays by Aborigines in Schools. Quiet specially for the aborigines, as
at Wauchope -- they would of course leave the furniture in the same
state as when found. The writer is a half caste" Forwarded for the
consideration of the Minister of Public Instruction from APB --
Forwarded to The Inspector General of Police

Note to Chief Inspector "The school building for Aborigines at Wauchope
was provided by the Minister. In view of Clause 4 of the Regulations I
recommend that the above application be declined."

> *Protests/resistance to school*
>
> 25/7/1892 -- Letter re attendance of Aboriginal children at Public
> School "Sir, I have the honor to report that the accompanying letter
> was handed to me this morning by Mr. Wallace. By the letter you will
> see that the residents object to send their children to this school
> while the blacks attend it. The fact of the aboriginal children
> attending has been a source of unpleasantness ever since my arrival at
> Wauchope. They (the aboriginal children) are inspected with the other
> children, as to cleanliness, twice every school day, and as far as
> outward appearance goes, there is little to complain of, but, there is
> always a very objectionable smell arising from them. The total
> enrolment for the week ending 22 July was 96 of whom 8 are aborigines.
> The average attendance for the same week was 83, including an average
> attendance of 4.9 aborigines. The attendance today was 9 white
> children and 6 aboriginal -- 2 of the latter attending today for the
> first time this quarter, making a total enrolment of 10 blacks. Will
> you please advise me what steps, if any, I shall take in this matter."
> Signed R. Hopper Teacher to Inspector Nolan Port Macquarie. Note
> "Please inform the residents that a contract has already been entered
> into for the provision of a separate school for aborigines, and that
> the building will be completed in three weeks. Please sign and return
> the papers." Papers signed and returned by teacher Hopper. Additional
> notes "How is the matter now progressing?" "Sir, I have the honor to
> state that manly all the children have retuned. The Aborigines still
> come. The new building for the latter is finished with the exception
> of chimney, shingling and hanging of door and setting windows. It is
> expected that the building will be quite finished today or
> tomorrow......."
>
> 25/7/1892 -- Letter to Mr. Nolan School Inspector Port Macquarie re
> attendance of Aboriginal children at Wauchope school "Sir, At a
> meeting held in ......(?) Hall on Saturday last parents of children
> attending the Wauchope Public School .... (?) was proposed and carries
> that on and after Monday, today they would not allow their children to
> attend the above school until the black children now attending school
> be ... (?) there from as they are not in a condition of cleanliness to
> mix with other children." Signed ... (?) Chairman of meeting
