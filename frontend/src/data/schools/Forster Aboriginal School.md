2/12/1890 -- Attendance of Aboriginal children at Public Schools -- 1890
-- Inspector Nolan's Report upon matters at Forster, Pelican Island,
Rollands Plains, Summer Island and Wauchope. Recommendation that
separate Schools for Aboriginals should be established as follows:
Provisional School at Forster H.T School at Wauchope and Rollands Plains
and Prov. School on Blacks reserve between Pelican Islands and Summer
Island Schools.

20/2/1894 -- Provisional School Forster Representing difficulty in
keeping up the attendance (Letter sent on to Police Department Sydney,
Inspector Generals Office) "I beg to state that four of the children who
went away during the children who went away during the Christmas
vacation have returned; and by making full inquiries as to the others,
there is no certainly about them coming back. The children here at
present are indifferent in attending school. I do my utmost to persuade
them to attend, but the parents do not seem to care, it seems to me as
if the children have complete control over the parents. The Constable
often warns them about coming to school but they don't seem to take much
notice." Signed John Scott. CI -- Recommended, that the Inspector
General of Police be asked to instruct the Constable at Forster to
endeavor to compel these children to attend. If they don't attend
better, the School must soon be closed.

24/3/1894 -- "Constable Fraser respectfully reports for the information
of his Superintendent. Re attached letter, that there are only two
half-caste children at Forster of School age, the others are living
about Taree who had previously attended the Aborigines school at Forster
their parents having removed from Forster an account of not being able
to get work, five of the children will be going away with their parents,
who are now attending the school, their father not being able to get any
work. The Constable has done his utmost to make the children attend
school, but their parents take no interest in sending the children to
school very few would attending only for receiving Government rations."
Signed John Fraser Constable.

31/5/1892 -- Forster Aboriginal -- Rent of Schoolroom.

Memorandum to Chief Inspector as to Provision of building for Aboriginal
School -- "I beg to inform you that on a recent visit to Forster I made
further inquiry in regard to this matter. I find that an enrolment of 15
may be expected, owing to the migratory habits of the blacks. I do not
consider it advisable to recommend the erection of noted buildings, but
I have ascertained that a suitable building can be rented. The building
referred to is a five roomed cottage the property of Mr. Henry Miles, of
Forster, and it can be rented at 6 shillings per week for a term of one
or more years. One of the rooms after the removal of a partition (which
Mr. Miles undertakes to effect) will measure 25 ft \* 12ft \* 9 ft....

1895 -- Tenders for construction of building -- cost of the building
should be about £360.0.0

8/1/1898 -- "Memo to Chief Inspector re Foster (Aborigines) Recommending
that the school be worked half-time. The enrolment and average
attendance last quarter at Forster were respectively 18 and 11.5, and at
Wallingat 15 and 12.2. The prospects of both schools are very poor, and
I recommend that they are worked together as Half Time Schools. The
distance between them is about 7 miles." Submitted and approved.

30/91 -- Letter from George Cross Teacher "Dear Sir, I do myself the
honor of returning you my sincere thanks for your kindness in helping me
to my present appointment but finding the very strong and disagreeable
smell arising from the Aboriginals is too much for me, and so have
forwarded my resignation Again thanking you and wishing you the
compliments of the coming season."

To Chief Inspector -- 23/5/1895 -- The teacher's schools are about 7
miles apart. He has not to use a horse, but is compelled to keep a boat
to row from school to school. The experience of purchasing a boat and
keeping it in repair is considerable, therefore I recommend that the
usual allowance (£10 per annum) be granted to Mr. Campbell from 1^st^
January last." Allowance granted

18/1/1893 -- small attendance at Aboriginal school here at South
Forster. All the school children except two have left the district
during the Christmas vacation, out of two remaining only one attends.

31/1/1893 -- respecting dimished attendance -- "During last week I went
to the trouble to try and bring them back from the Manning River, I
could only get two of them to come, the others have no intention of
coming back." Signed John Scott

5/2/1894 -- Reporting small attendance -- "Sir, I beg to state that the
total average attendance for the last three weeks was only 2.6. This
being due to nearly all the children leaving the district during the
Christmas vacation.
