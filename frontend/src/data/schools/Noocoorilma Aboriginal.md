20/7/1916 -- Miss. Eva. Hagan Teacher in charge -- Application for a
school Press for the use at Sevington school. "This is now the third
time I have made application, previous applications being in February
and May." Note from Chief Inspector -- If Press is needed at the above
school If granted it should be forwarded by rail to Inverell.

12/12/1916 -- Application for permission to change the name of the
school -- " I have the honor to apply for permission to change the name
of the above school, as there are 2 schools in Sevington, viz --
Aboriginal Provisional School and the Subisided School at Green Hills
Sevington, and the mail of each is constantly being sent to the other. I
would suggest that in future the Aboriginal School be called by the
native name of the Station, viz -- Noocoorilma Provisional Sevington Via
Wellingrove and Glen Innes." Note from Chief Inspector -- The Aborigines
Provisional School now officially known as Sevington is situated on the
Mission Reserve, Noocoorilma. Sevington is the name of some adjoining
property. I recommend that the name of the school be changed from
Sevington to Noocoorilma."

Note attached -- "Perhaps a secondary reason that could be advanced is
the desirability of retaining native names for Native schools. The word
Noocoorilma means the Native Apple Tree." Teacher Eve. Hagan

9/2/1917 -- I have to inform you that approval has been given to the
change of name of the school under your charge to Noocoorilma. You
should make the necessary alterations in the school records and
documents.

6/6/1917 -- Miss. Eva Hagan Teacher in Charge of Sevington School --
Application for a School Press.

14/2/1918 -- Memo from Teacher Mr. Burrage -- Re: closing of School --
"Request advise concerning holidays (if any) granted to the schools in
this district (Inverell) for Inverell show week. Should there be none
granted to schools attended by White children can an exception be made
in the case of the above Aboriginal School. I ask because it is the
intention of all the families on this Station to be in Inverell that
week whether holidays are granted or not. Note from Chief Inspector --
Noocoorilma Aboriginal Station is 32 miles from Inverell. The show is
being held on 27^th^ 28^th^ February and 1^st^ March. Last year the
aboriginals from the settlement visited the show. I recommend that the
closing of the school on 28^th^ February and 1^st^ March be sanctioned.

30/1/1919 -- Non-attendance of children at School -- failure of water.
Memo from Teacher Mr. Burrage -- "I have the honor to report that there
has been no attendance of children at the above school for the first
three days of the new school year, Jan 20^th^, 21^st^ and 22^nd^. Owing
to the drying up of the drinking wells, a water-famine is being
experienced, and the Aborigines resident on the Station have been
compelled to move camp to some permanent water, some miles away. It is
there intention to return as soon as the drought conditions after and
water again becomes available."

19/2/1919 -- Absence of Scholars on account of water-famine. Memo from
Teacher in Charge -- Mr. Burrage -- I have the honor to further report
that the water famine on the Sevington (Noocoorilma) Aboriginal Station
still continues, and that the families usually resident here, are all
still camped some miles away at the river. As a consequence there is no
attendance at the School. My first communication with reference to this
matter, addressed to Mr. Inspector Clemens, Inverell, not having been
acknowledged. I am doubtful as to whether you are acquainted with the
position of affairs here. Hence this second report. I earnestly hope
that a good fall of rain soon ends the trouble."

16/3/1919 -- Letter to APB from Dept. Ed. -- "with reference to a report
received from the Inspector of Schools at Inverell respecting the
non-attendance of children at the Aboriginal School at Noocoorilma, I
should be glad if you would inform me whether existing conditions at the
Aboriginal Station are likely to continue in order that arrangements may
be made for the continuance of the school or otherwise."

APB -- 24/3/1919 -- RE School for Aborigines Noocoorilma "With reference
to your communication of the 6^th^ instant, No.7259, regarding the
non-attendance of children at the Aborigines' School Noocoorilma, - I
have now to advise you that Mr. Inspector Swindlehurst of this
Department has just returned from a visit thereto, and the enrolment is
now 19, all of whom were attending the School on the date of the
Inspector's visit, and after Easter this number will probably increase
to 23 or probably 24. Under the circumstances, I do not doubt for one
moment that your Department will continue this school." Note from
Department Ed. Inform that, in view of the additional facts furnished in
connection with the increased attendance at Noocoorilma it is not
proposed to close the provisional schools there.

APB -- 25/9/1923 -- to Dept. Education -- "School Material on Hand at
Sevington (Noocoorilma) Reserve near Wellingrove. I beg to inform you
that there is a quantity of School material at the School building on
the Aborigines' Reserve at Sevington near Wellingrove. As it is proposed
to sell the building for removal I shall be glad if you will arrange to
have same taken charge of as early as possible. I would suggest that you
communicate with the Officer in charge of Police, Ashford, under whose
supervision the Reserve now is."

31/10/1923 -- Letter from Dept. Education to APB -- Subject: School
material at Noocoorilma (Sevington) -- "In reply to your letter above
mentioned, I desire to inform you that the local Inspector of Schools
will arrange to have the school materials removed from the Noocoorilma
(Sevington) building to the Strathbogie Provisional School.

18/9/1918 -- Memo from Teacher Mr. Burrage -- Re: Sanctioned requested
for closing school for one week on account of wife's illness, and
application for a further weeks leave of absence. "I have the honor to
advise that I have found it necessary to close the School under my
charge during this week ... My wife, since her recent confinement, has
been ordered complete rest be her Doctor until such time as she regains
the use of her limbs. She is now improving, but she requires constant
attention. Up until Monday last, her mother was with her but was then
obliged to return home, and then, owing to the inability of securing the
necessary extra help with the house and children, and as the Doctor
would not sanctioned moving Mrs. Burrage back to the Hospital in
Inverell owing to the thirty mile drive, I have been compelled to take
up nursing of my wife, and the care of three children (eldest four
years) myself. Miss. Malcom, the missionary on this Station, gives what
time she can to the baby, but is so situated that she is unable to do
more than just that. Should assistance be procurable, or should my wife
make speedy recovery, I would immediately resume my duties, but in the
meantime, I beg to request the sanction of the Department for my action
in closing the School this week, and to apply for leave of absence next
week. In considering my request, I trust that our complete isolation at
Sevington will be remembered."

Memo from Teacher -- 8/4/1918 -- Closing of School at Noocoorilma from
8^th^ April until 13^th^ April -- " I have the honor to report that I
have found it necessary to close the above Aboriginal School for the
week commencing 8^th^ April on account of an outbreak somewhat
resembling Chicken Pox. Every child attending the School is affected
with sores, some being severe cases. I am treating all the children
personally at the Manager's residence with medicine and ointment and
hope to resume school a the beginning of next week." Approved.

24/4/1918 -- Reply from Chief Inspector -- Your letter of the 8^th^
instant reporting the closing of your school on account of an outbreak
somewhat resembling Chicken Pox -- With reference to the above I have to
inform you that if every child was affected with chicken pox there was
nothing else to do but close the school. On the other hand as all
children were infected they might be allowed to resume attendance as
they become physically fit in spite of anything to the contrary in
Circular No. 6 (amended re-issue. This memorandum should be signed by
you and forwarded to your Inspector without delay." J. Dawson Chief
Inspector
