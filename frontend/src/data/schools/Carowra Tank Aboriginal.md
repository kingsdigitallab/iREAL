27/10/1908 -- Carowra -- "Inquiry re school for aborigines -- Chief

APB -- 14/12/1908 -- "Sir, I have the honor, by direction, to ask that
the Minister of Public Instruction may be pleased to consider the
desirableness of establishing a school at Carowra Tank, near Mossgiel,
where an Aboriginal settlement has been formed, and where there are said
to be twenty-eight (28) children between the ages of 5 and 15 years,
including three white children. 2. I am to explain that, some time
since, the Board appointed a Mr. William McDonald for the purpose of
looking after the Aborigines of the District and undertaking their
primary instruction, and he reports that there are now 93 Aborigines on
the settlement and 52 of them (in addition to the white children
referred to) are being instructed by him. 3. My Board have extended Mr.
McDonald's employment for a period of three months from the 7^th^
November, ultimo, but consider that the time has now arrived when a
school should be established under the Department of Public
Instruction."

10/12/1925 -- (Further notes listed under) -- APB -- appointment of Mr.
C.P. Cherbury as Teacher -- Manager of the Aboriginal Reserve at Carowra
Tank. In accordance with the instructions of my Board I beg to forward
herewith application by Mr. C. P. Cherbury for the position of
Teacher-Manager of the Aborigines Reserve at Carowra Tank via Mossgiel,
and to recommend that subject to Mr. Cherbury being considered suitable
by your District School Inspector at Cowra, he be appointed to the
position at a salary to be fixed by your Department. It might be
explained that Carowra Tank is the centre of a large tribe of Aborigines
and is situated about 50 miles from Trida Station Homestead. Sometime
ago the Board erected a Teacher's residence and School on the Reserve
and arranged for two female missionaries to reside there and carry on
the school work. This experiment was not successful, however, owing, it
is thought to the isolation of the place and to certain personal
differences between the Missionaries. Mr. and Mrs. Cherbury are both
perfectly aware of the conditions under which they would have to work
and are fully prepared to accept same being themselves imbued with a
Missionary spirit. Their present address is Cowra Post Office. PS: It is
estimated that there would be an attendance of forty pupils at the
Carowra Tank School.

Employment of Mr. C.P Cherbury as teacher of the Aborigines School at
Carowra Tank. "The Secretary of the Aborigines Protection Board
nominated Mr. Cherbury as the most suitable man for this position. It is
one calling for special personal qualities and Mr. Pettit strongly
recommends Mr. Cherbury's appointment. -- Carowra Tank is in the centre
of a large tribe of Aborigines and is situated about 50 miles from Trida
Station Homestead. The Secretary states that the Board has experienced
great difficulty in getting a suitable man for the position. The
Employment committee considered the question but were unable to
recommend Mr. Cherbury's appointment owing to his age (62). However, in
the special circumstances of this case, the matter of Mr. Cherbury's age
should not be a barrier to his employment. I have seen Mr. Cherbury and
have no hesitation in recommending his appointment. I should be glad if
an early decision could be given in this matter. -- Approved -- Refer to
Public Service Board -- The Public Service Board approve.

1/5/1909 -- Establishment of School for Aborigines. Department of Public
Instruction "As a result of inquiries I have made into this matter I beg
to submit the following report: - (1) Carowra Tank is 60 miles from
Mossgiel. A Sulky which is once a week the only means of communication
with the place. There is no accommodation on the route, and no
accommodation for a teacher, could be found at the Tank except the tents
used by the Aborigines. The nearest residence of white people (except
possibly that of one man who has married a black women) as Kajuligah
Station, homestead 16 miles distance, and Moolah Station 12 miles
distant. (2) the late teacher Mr. Macdonald who is reported to have
become eccentric left the tank some time ago. (3) I am informed that
rations have been withdrawn from about 50% of the Aborigines congregated
at the tanks, that in consequence many are leaving with their families,
and the camp is gradually breaking up. (4) The general opinion amongst
those competent to form an opinion is that if the Mission were removed
to the immediate vicinity of Ivanhoe there would be some prospect of its
stability. The women and children could be protected, and the men could
support their families by working on the neighboring stations. If some
such scheme were adopted and the mission located near some town it would
be practicable to provide means of education for the children. In very
of these facts. I recommend that the Secretary of the AP. Board be
informed that it is not practicable to establish a School at Carowra
Tank.

3/11/1932 -- Carowa Tank Public Aborigines School -- Additional
accommodation -- Chief Inspectors report of inspection of station. "I
have to state that at my recent visit of inspection to the above school,
I found the accommodation inadequate and otherwise unsatisfactory. There
are 48 children in attendance and the Teacher says that there are about
ten children between the ages of six and seven who are available for
attendance but that there is not sufficient room for them. The present
school building is a construction of wood with galvanized iron walls and
roof. It is provided with only two small windows which are closed with
shutters and are not glazed. The dimensions of the room are 29' \* 12'
which it will be seen do not conform to the requirements of more than 36
pupils. The ventilation is not satisfactory. Even on a cold day I found
the atmosphere in the school most oppressive and unpleasant. The
conditions in hot weather must be unbearable. The population of this
settlement seems to be on the increase. The total number of children
between the ages of two and fourteen years is 92. It therefore appears
to be a matter of urgency that more adequate provision should be made in
the school accommodation. I recommend that this matter be brought before
the Board for the Protection of Aborigines requesting that suitable and
sufficient accommodation be provided." Note -- Send copy of this report
to Aborigines Protection Board, for consideration.

20/4/1933 -- Memo from teacher Carowra Tank to Department Education re:

accommodation at this school entirely inadequate for the number of
pupils and in order to carry on with the work I have enclosed a portion
of the verandah of the school and am now making use of it as a
classroom. I trust that my action in this matter may have your approval.
-- Noted by Chief Inspector -- Forwarded. The accommodation of this
school I presume a mater for the Board for the Protection of Aborigines.
The matter was reported after my inspection last year.
