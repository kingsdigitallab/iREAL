**Role of the Department and relationship to the APB**

14/9/1900 -- APB to Dept. Public Instruction -- Minister of Public
Instruction is desirous of establishing a School for the children of the
Aborigines residing in the locality of Burnt Bridge and asking that my
Board arrange for the erection of a suitable building for the purpose

Chief Inspector -- 12/3/1901 -- "I have again visited Burnt Bridge to
inquire as to the proposals of the place. The three families who were
living there when I last visited the place are still there and have 11
children over 5 years of age to attend school - the two families who
were supposed to intend to settle there have no done so, and I think it
is very doubtful if they will do so. The prospects are less encouraging
then they were, and I recommend that the previous offer be withdrawn,
and that a school be not established at Burnt Bridge."

19/4/1900 -- "Sir, We have the honor to direct your attention to the
fact that there are resident on Euroka Creek in the locality of "Burnt
Bridge" Parish of Kallatina Count of Dudley about 5 families of
Aboriginals comprised in which are our 20 children of school age. These
children might receive instruction at the Euroka Public School but all
who presented themselves at that school were sent back to their homes.
We respectfully ask that provision be made for their instruction by the
establishment of a Provisional School on the Government Land reserved as
an Aboriginal settlement and the appointment of a teacher. If this is
done we feel assured that other Aboriginal families will come to reside
in the locality and thus the number of children will be increased."
Signed by 9 Aboriginal people from the area.

30/4/1901 -- Letter to Dept Public Instruction from George Davis of
Burnt Bridge -- "With reference to the school at Burnt Bridge I would
like to Bring it, before your notice that the whole affair seems to be
forgotten since the Block of Land has been measured out for the purpose,
we would like to urge you to see into it so there is a number of
children will seen be over age if not soon delt with we trust dear sir
you will move in the matter at once the number of children that are
prepared for the present school in question you will find in the next
page."

27/6/1900 -- Memo to Chief Inspector -- Summary report on Application
for the establishment of a Provisional School for Aborigines and
Recommendation -- "Five families of Aborigines have small farms on
Euroka Creek, about 3 miles from Kempsey, but at the time of my visit
two of these families were away at Rollands Plains. There are in all 20
children to attend school, and they all live within half a mile of a
central site, but owing to the wandering habits of the race, I am
doubtful if the average attendance for a Provisional School can be
maintained. As however, the children are not allowed to attend Euroka
School, I think provision should be made for them, and therefore
recommend: - (1) that a Provisional School for Aborigines be established
at Burnt Bridge, (2) That the site described in accompanying papers be
secured (3) That a building (similar to that at the Hatch) be erected at
a cost of £40 to £50.

29/9/1903 -- To the Chairman of the Public School Board -- "Dear Sir, I
beg to draw your attention to the fact that there are a number of
half-caste children at Burnt Bridge for whom no provision for schooling
is made. There are 16 children between the age of 5 and 15, who would
attend if a school was established. I enclose a list of their names and
ages. Trusting the Board will ensure in the matter without delay."

4/3/1916 -- Letter from Mabel Johnson "As the AP Board have approved of
my transfer to Nulla Nulla, I am unable to accede to their request owing
to climatic and other reasons and I am therefore compelled to resign
unless another transfer is given."

22/3/1916 -- APB "As the Board desire to appoint a Teacher-Manager at
Burnt Bridge, with a view to the better supervision and cultivation of
the Reserve, it became necessary to suggest the transfer of Miss
Johnston to Bellbrook, as it is the only suitable vacancy to which she
could be appointed. Unless she is willing to accept that transfer, I am
afraid her resignation will have to be accepted."

28/5/1938 -- Classification of School -- "As the enrolment at my school
has now reached 51 pupils and my last term's return showed an average
attendance of 43.3 and taking into consideration that an assistant
teacher has been appointed, may consideration be given to raising the
classification from V1 to V lass please."

31/7/1912 -- APB to Dept Public Instruction -- "I beg to inform you that
my Board have appointed Miss. E.M.F. Rutter, Teacher of the Aborigines
School at Burnt Bridge, near Kempsey, to the position of Matron in

21/9/1912 -- Police Station Kempsey -- Applications by Misses Mabel
Johnson, and Gertrude J. Walker for the position of Teacher at the
Aborigines School at Burnt Bridge "I beg to report that after
interviewing both the above-named candidates, I am of opinion, Miss
Mabel Johnson is the more suitable. She is 28 years of age, and had had
12 months experience of the Aborigines as Missionary. Eight months of
this time she spent among them at Bellbrook assisting the Teacher, Miss
Smith, in imparting instruction to the children. Shortly after Miss
Johnson's arrival at Bellbrook she started a class for washing, ironing,
and sewing, and in this connection has been very successful, and seems
to manage Aborigines children very well. Should she receive the
appointment applied for, she will reside with the Missionary at Burnt
Bridge, and so save the Board £1 per week, - cost of conveyance to and
from the Reserve, Miss Walker had no experience of Aborigines."

22/11/1909 -- Appointment of Miss. E.M. F. Rutter as Teacher

4/10/1919 -- APB -- Forwarded for information. It is presumed that a
fresh appointment to the vacancy at Burnt Bridge, Kempsey, will have to
be made forthwith. A Miss Trevaskis, who is at present the Teacher of
the Subsidised School at Trunkey Creek, called upon me a few days ago
and expressed a desire to secure an appointment to an Aborigines School.
Under the circumstances, it may be considered desirable to give her the
vacancy at Kempsey."

Reply from APB -- "With reference to your communication ... I desire to
inform you that, in view of the Public Service Board's decision that no
person over the age of 40 years is eligible for admission to the Public
Service Board, Miss Trevaskis cannot be appointed to the position in
question as she is 42 years of age."

Application from Miss. Elsie. Violet Herbert -- Aged 34 as Teacher at

> *Students -- conditions of school building*

1925 -- Burnt Bridge Accommodation -- "The present accommodation is
inadequate. Thirty-seven pupils are enrolled and, for these, there is a
floor space of 252 square feet. As the attendance fluctuates
considerably, it is difficult to recommend any expenditure in the
direction of enlargements.

Letter from Presbyterian Minister -- 28/3/1925 re accommodation at the
school. Minister has been visiting the school for a number of years and
the school is becoming more cramped. Reply from Department "Rev, Sir,
With reference to your letter dated 28^th^ March last asking that
additional accommodation be provided at the abovementioned school, I
have to inform you that overcrowding at this school was partly due to
the attendance of aborigines who had wandered from their regular
reserve. Arrangements have now been made, however, with the Aborigines
Protection Board for the removal of the Aborigines concerned which will
remedy the conditions complained of by you. I have to state further that
additional seating accommodation has also be placed in the school."

5/5/1925 -- Extract from Sanitary report -- School Building inadequate
space. Ventilation poor, hat pegs in schoolroom insufficient, school
buildings old and in bad repair, long forms and desks used. Water Supply
-- No lavatory accommodation. Sanitary arrangements -- Latrines require
screens and hinged lids, A boys urinal is also needed. Poor sanitary
condition -- pans are open at front.

> *Living conditions*

Medical Officers report . Memo to Chief Inspector.
[Accommodation] -- This matter has been reported. The
overcrowding is due to unusual circumstances and, as the contributing
causes may cease at any time, no expenditure in the way of enlargement
can be recommended. [Outhouses] -- The general sanitary
condition of the Camp is poor. As a matter of fact, the question of
removing the whole settlement has been under consideration. In the
circumstances the AP Board has made reasonable provision for sanitary
requirements by erecting two outhouses (pan system). The provision of
screens, lids and a urinal would involve an expense hardly warranted in
the special conditions of an aborigines settlement. I shall ask the
teacher to have the missing boards replaced, the elder boys do rough
carpentry.
