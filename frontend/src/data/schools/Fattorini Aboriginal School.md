**Role of the Department and relationship to the APB**

11/4/1911 -- Dept. Public Instruction -- Acting Under Secretary --
"Recommended that Mr. Terry a Missionary under the auspices of the
Australian Aborigines Mission, be appointed as temporary teacher of the
half time schools for Aborigines at Fatrorini and Pelican Islands, with
salary at the rate of £100 per annum, payable from the date of his entry
on duty, on the understanding that this temporary employment will give
him no claim for another appointment in the service of this Department."

6/2/1911 -- APB -- Respecting appointment of teacher and supply of
school material. A suitable building has now been erected at Pelican
Island.

2/1911 -- Copy of Precis submitted to Board on the 2/2/1911 -- Half Time
School for Pelican and Fattorini Islands, Lower Macleay Island. "Towards
the end of last year the Department of Public Instruction intimated they
were prepared to establish a half-time school at Pelican and Fattorini
Islands, where there are about 17 or 18 children of school age, if a
building were erected at the latter place, - there being already a
suitable building at Fattorini Island. A school building was erected by
the Board at a cost of £35.15.0 and an application is now submitted by
Mr. and Mrs. Terry (husband and wife, who are Missionaries under the
auspices of the Australian Aborigines Mission) for the position of
Teachers at these places. They also submit a list of requirements for
the school buildings. Perhaps the application might be forwarded for
favor of consideration by the Department of Public Instruction."

2/4/1910 -- Letter to the Under Secretary Dept. Public Instruction --
"The mission council represent that there are about 40 children on the
two islands. Of these, the Police Report shows that 14 are between the
age of six and fourteen. Probably there are a number of others who would
attend the school if established. It appears that a school building is
already provided at Fattorini Island. I recommend that the Aborigines
Protection Board be informed that in the event of a suitable school room
being provided at Pelican Island, this Department is prepared to
establish a Half Time School. Approved.

29/1/1910 -- Police Station Gladstone -- "Application for a Teacher for
Aborigines Fatorina Island and Pelican Island and Kinchela, Aborigines
Reserve -- Constable Moore No 6497 begs to report, that there are (17)
seventeen Aborigine children between the ages of six and fourteen years
on the various Aborigines Reserves on the Lower Macleay River, which are
within a radius of ten miles. They are divided as follows, Fatorina 5
girls and 2 boys, on Kinchela, 2 girls and 1 boy, and on Pelican Island.
1 girl and 6 boys, the two Islands are about ten miles apart with
Kinchela about half way between the two.'

19/12/1918 -- Dept. Education -- Re Closing of Fattorini Island and
Conversion of Pelican Island to Full Time. "Fattorini Island and Pelican
Island Half Time Aboriginal Schools -- I found when visiting Fattorini
Island Aboriginal School that the intended companion half-time school at
Pelican Island had not yet been opened. The teacher informed me that it
was because there was no school material at Pelican Island. He is a
returned soldier but was not formerly a teacher and did not know it was
necessary to report the mater. Fattorini Island was worked as full-time.
The present enrolment there is 13 and only 5 are expected after the
vacation. I recommend that Fattorini Island be closed and Pelican
Island, where an enrolment of 20 is expected be opened full time. The
school material of Fattorini Island can be transferred to the Pelican
Island school." Submitted. Recommendation Approved.

17/1/1919 -- Dept. Education to APB -- "I have to acquaint you that in
view of the small attendance at the Fattorini Island Half Time
Aboriginal School, the Minister for Education has decided to close that
school and to work the companion school at Pelican Island as a Full-time
school.

5/7/1918 -- Dept Education -- Fattorini Aboriginal School --
Recommending appointment of Mr. Thomas. L. Watson as Teacher. "There is
a vacancy as a Teacher at Fattorini (Pelican Island), for which Mr.
Watson has been recommended by the Aborigines Protection Board. Mr.
Watson is a returned soldier. I have interviewed him, and consider that
he would satisfactorily fill the position of Teacher of an aboriginal
school. I recommend that Mr. Watson be deemed eligible for employment as
Teacher of an aboriginal school, with salary at the minimum rate of £132
per annum, on the understanding that such appointment will give him no
claim to any other position in the teaching service other than that of
Teacher of an aboriginal school."

29/10/1918 -- Dept. Education "Forage Allowance -- In the case of these
aborigines schools the teacher occupies a residence provided by the
aborigines Protection Board, midway between the schools, and is
accordingly required to drive 5 miles each way to and from either
school. From the tenor of his application it is also apparent that the
teacher also uses his horse and vehicle or inspection work for the
Board. While the teacher has made out a clear case for an allowance, I
do not think that this Department should be expected to incur
expenditure. The schools are for aborigines and it seems to me a matter
for the Board to adjust. I recommend (1) That the aborigines Protection
Board be asked to consider the case with a view to granting the teacher
an allowance, and (2) That the Board be informed that if the case were
one for this Department's responsibility the teacher would be eligible
for an allowance of £15 per annum for forage in connection with his
school duties alone. The fact that he has other journeys in addition
might be considered by the Board with the view to granting more than £15
under such conditions."

26//6/1918 -- Letter to Mr. Thomas Lauder Watson -- "Sir, I have the
honor to inform you that your employment as Temporary Teacher of the
Aborigines School at Fattorini (Pelican Island), has been recommended.
This school is under the supervision of Mr. T. Sheedy, B. A. Inspector
of Schools, whose official address is Kempsey. It will be your duty to
communicate with him upon all matters relating to the school under your
charge and to your position as teacher. Salary at the rate of £132 per
annum will be paid to you by this Department from the date of your entry
on duty. That date should be notified to your Inspector as soon as
practicable after you commence duty. It is to be distinctly understood
that this appointment will give you no claim to any position under this
Department other than that of Teacher of an aboriginal school. At the
expiration of a fortnight after commencing your duties you should
proceed to the Kinchela Creek Public School and spend a week thereat
observing teaching methods. The Teacher of that school will be notified
of this arrangement."

Experience

26/6/1918 -- Mr. Watson -- Education at Fort Street. -- 4 years -- 1^st^
class in those days. Was in Tramway service about 10 years. Was a
Soldier 2 years 286 days in German New Guinea. Discharge seen as
satisfactory. Has had no teaching experience. After a fortnight at
Pelican Island he should spend a week at Kinchela Creek School. "I have
a fair knowledge of Agriculture and a First class knowledge of
Horticulture and Poultry. Had a few months experience with sheep on
Charton Station near Bourke. Had a little fresh Carpentry and fencing to
do on above station. Prior to enlisting was in business for myself and
kept my own books. Education with a Fifth Class form at the Fort. St.
Model School."

1/10/1913 -- Mr. Terry writing to Dept. Public Instruction "On Tuesday
Sept. 9^th^ my presence was required at Nurse Boughton's Private
Hospital Kempsey, on account of the serious illness of my daughter. I
returned to Gladstone on Wednesday evening intending to resume my duties
at Fattorini Island Thursday Sept. 11^th^, but on my way to school, was
again recalled to the hospital, and my baby died on Thursday evening.
Friday was taken up with making arrangements for the removal of the body
to "Yulgilbar" and it was necessary for me to go to Sydney to complete
arrangements for the burial of my daughter in the family grave at
Waverly."

**Community**

*11/1/1910 -- APB -- "Sir, when on a visit to Fatorini Island some time
ago, I was asked by the people there to try and get a teacher for their
children. The Missionary Mr. Huxley, has been doing what he could to
teach them, but as he has four other reserves to attend to, he has no
time to do much for them. The matter was discussed in our Aborigines
Mission Local Council, and it was decided to place the matter before
you. The Missionary was asked to find out how many children of a School
age were on the Island. When the Pelican Island people heard it they
wanted a teacher also, so we thought in case a teacher was sent it might
be possible to have a half-time school, the Islands being 10 miles
apart. There are 9 (nine) children on each Island, with more to follow
there being just on 40 (forty) children altogether on both Islands. We
have a Church on Fattorini Island which could be used for a school, it
is fitted up with desks and seats all ready for use. There is no
suitable building on Pelican Island. "*
