**Role of the Department and relationship to the APB**

3/8/1899 -- Mulyan Provisional -- As to change of name -- "As there is a
Public School in operation in the Wellington district known as Mulyan,
will you be good enough to submit a recommendation as to the alteration
of the designation of the Provisional School at Mulyan under your
supervision." Note from Chief Inspector -- "I recommend that the School
be known as Erambie Provisional." - Erambie is an Aboriginal term for a
water-hole.

12/1/1900 -- APB -- "I am directed to acknowledge the receipt of your
letter dated 29^th^ instant.. and to state that my Board have issued
instructions for the effecting of certain necessary repairs to the
Provisional School for Aborigines at Erambie near Cowra.

18/10/1905 -- "Sir, I am directed to acknowledge the receipt of your
communication of the 10^th^ instant, respecting an allowance of £25 per
annum, paid by the Aborigines Protection Board to Mr. B. Morgan, teacher
of Provisional School at Erambie, and to state that this matter of
receiving consideration, and recommendation as to the continuance of the
allowance is being forwarded to you. The allowance was granted by the
Aborigines Protection Board on account of the special conditions, and
owing to the fact that, at the time, the salary paid by this Department
was about £50 per annum only." Reply -- "Mr. Balfour Morgan, teacher of
the above school, received £88 per annum from this Department and I
understand an additional £25 p.a from the Aborigines Protection Board.
Has this latter been approved by the Public Service Board? And is it
intended that the arrangement will continue as regards to any teacher
appointed to Erambie?" Note from Accountant -- Ascertain whether the
Teacher has any duties to carry out for the Aborigines Protection Board
outside his duties as teacher?

26/10/1894 - £25 from APB towards salary of teacher.

31/10/1894 -- "Mulyan Provisional for Aborigines Cowra -- I am unable to
nominate teacher perhaps the candidate whose application is with former
papers on subject is eligible still advisable."

Letter from Chief Inspector 25/9/1894 -- Reporting that the School has
not been in operation since 3^rd^ instant. Teacher Mr Chazelle. The
question of re-opening the school. "Some two weeks ago I reported this
circumstances by telegram and promised to furnish further particulars in
due course. I visited the place today and made inquiries 1. on the 3^rd^
instant, the teacher told his pupils that he was leaving, as he had
received an appointment in Sydney. He left the district that night, and
has not yet returned. As I have not been notified of any leave having
been granted to him, I presume that he has absconded, as he did in the
beginning of the year. HE told some persons in Cowra, that he had been
appointed to Wollongong. 2. The parents of the Aboriginal children say
that he seldom opened the school before 10 o'clock. 3. The instructional
results at last inspection were very inferior, he was called upon for an
explanation and warned. 4. Altogether, his record is a bad one. 5. There
are now 12 children of school age on the Mulyan Mission Station and they
live within a few hundred yards of the school. So far this quarter , the
average is 10. The Aborigines are well behaved, have supported the
school well, and are very anxious to have it re-opened. It is possible
that new arrivals from other localities will settle down at the station.
I recommend: - that Mr. Chas Lazelle be dismissed the service, if absent
without leave or sufficient cause. That the school be re-opened at H to
H rates and that the Aborigines Protection Board be asked to contribute
(say £25 per year) towards a teachers salary. (The position is a trying
one and should receive a special salary).

Letter from Mr Lazelle asking for reinstatement to position -- Comments
from Under Secretary "Mr Lazelle's place at Mulyan has not been filled
up. I recommend that he be severely reprimanded for his action and
cautioned as to his future conduct, and that he be allowed to return to
duty at Mulyan."

23/2/1894 -- Mulyan (Aboriginal) Stating that the Board are prepared to
nominate at teacher. APB "Sir, I am directed to inform you that the
Board are prepared to nominate a suitable person to carry on the duties
of teaching at the School for Aboriginal children at Mulyan, near Cowra,
in the place of Mr. C. Lazelle, who it is understood has been dismissed
by your Department. It has been reported to the Board that the School at
Mulyan has not been opened since Xmas vacation."

Letter to Department from Miss Marion Muir 8/2/1894 -- Requesting
re-employment as teacher of the Aboriginal School -- "The Public School
which was erected in 1893 on the reserve situated on the left bank of
the Lachlan river, just above Cowra, for the purpose of educating the
local blacks, is now apparently closed for want of a teacher. I reside
in close proximity to the school and hold a 3A certificate from the
Department of Public Instruction, having resigned six years ago, I have
the honor to make application for the position of teacher to the above
school."

28/11/1905 -- There was no attendance of pupils at this school on
account of a wedding on the Mission. The children attended the wedding.

21/8/1906 -- Teacher E. Griffiths -- "Sir, I have the honor to request
you to ask the chairman of the Public A Board to reconsider his decision
with regard to the payment of a supplementary allowance to me.
Considering the undesirable circumstances I am working under, and also
that my predecessors received an allowance I think it is only justice
for me to do the same. As teacher of this school I am continually
performing services for the people on the mission and hence ask for a
small remuneration for my time and labour." Reply -- This Department is
not prepared to recommend any higher salary to Mr. Griffiths as Teacher
of the School. If, however, he renders service outside his duties as a
Teacher, it rests with the Aborigines Protection Board to determine
whether any payment should be made on account of those services, and if
any, what amount. The previous decision of the Public Service Board was
given on the understanding that no such services were rendered." Reply
from APB -- "... However, as the Teacher has no duties to perform in
connection with the local Camp, I do not see any warrant for continuing
the allowance, but, before coming to a final decision, the matter might
perhaps be referred to the Department of Public Instruction."

11/5/1914 -- APB -- Vacancy for Teacher at Provisional School at
Erambie, near Cowra. "Through the transfer of Miss Eva Hagan from
Erambie to Sevington, a vacancy has occurred at the former School. Mr.
Shadrach L. James, late of Cumeroogunja, has applied for the position.
The salary pertaining to the position is £110 per annum." The Board for
the Protection of Aborigines recommend the appointment of Miss Muriel
Young, of "Belle-Vue" Queanbeyan, whose application is attached.
Shadrach James has been informed that his name will be considered in
connection with some future vacancy."

1914 -- Letter from Shadrack Livingstone James -- applying for position
as teacher. He goes on to write "I have been here for the past week,
preaching to the people, and my ambition is to follow fathers footsteps
to be a teacher, and a preacher, on one of these mission stations.

15/9/1919 -- Letter from Mrs. W. Hughson to APB -- "Sir, in taking the
liberty in addressing your Board, I would first of all thank you for
your kind letter received some time ago re the Influenza. I am a member
of the AIM and as such am of course intensely interested in both the
Spiritual and temporal welfare of our Aborigines. Since coming to Cowra
to live -- my husband being Traffic Inspection of GR I have worked
amongst the dark people here, but have at times been handicapped having
no place to hold services. Miss Shaw the School Mistress is very good
and allows me .. use of the school house but sometimes she is away when
I send for the keys and they unintentionally I mislead the people for I
have to forgo the meeting owing to being unable to gain admission to the
school. Would it be too much to ask the board if I could have a set of
keys for my own use. But better still, there is one house here vacant,
owing to a death during the influenza, could I have such for a mission
house, I am free to hold services every Sunday and often go over through
the week and I believe it would be for the good of the people if they
had a place set apart for service. The only thing necessary would be a
clean out ..."

28/11/1925 -- Dept. Education -- Re: Erambie (Aboriginal)-
Recommendation further extended leave without pay in the case of Mr. R.
Constable, Teacher -- Manager. "Mr. Constable was granted four months
leave of absence without pay from the 20^th^ April last to enable him to
complete a contract for the erection of six huts and a number of
outbuildings at the above school. The Secretary of the Aborigines
Protection Board has now advised that the work cannot be finished for
another three months and has asked that the necessary leave be granted
to Mr. Constable. His son will continue in charge of the school for this
period. Mr. Constable might be granted three months further leave
without pay from the above-mentioned date.

12/8/1927 -- Erambie -- Temporary release of Mr. R. C. Constable from
teaching duties at Erambie School. "I beg to advise that my Board is
desirous of arranging for the re-erection of the Manager's house on the
Aboriginal Station at Pilliga, which was originally erected by Mr. R. C.
Constable, who is at present Teacher-Manager of the Aborigines Reserve,
Erambie, near Cowra, and which was some time ago destroyed by fire.
Although every effort has been made by advertising in the press to
secure tenders for this work, the Board has been unsuccessful in
arranging a contract, and it is now proposed, with the concurrence of
your Department, to again entrust the work to Mr. Constable, who was
previously builder and contractor and who is willing to undertake same.
Mr. Constables duties would be temporarily taken over by his son Mr. R.
Constable, who occupied the position on a previous occasion at Pilliga,
while his father was erecting some buildings for the Board at Cowra.
Under the circumstances, therefore, perhaps formal approval might be
given for the arrangement of the father being simply transferred from
the Department's pay sheet to that of my Board."

APB -- 26/10/1938 -- Aborigines School, Erambie -- The Board's Inspector
in his report of a recent visit of inspection of the Aboriginal Station,
Cowra, stated that the average attendance at the School is 48 and in his
opinion the appointment of an assistant teacher is justified. The matter
is referred for favor of consideration. Such an officer, if appointed,
would not be an assistant to the present teacher in so far as his
managerial duties are concerned."

**Teachers**

3/11/1899 -- Application for Removal -- Mr Chas. G. Kable applying for
removal to a classified Public School. Teacher has 3A Certificate from
1^st^ July 1899. Has been 1 yr, 7 months in charge of this School which
"is an undesirable position". Previous to this teacher was teaching in
the Hay district "and the successive heat for three summers has somewhat
affected my health. I am a married man; I therefore beg to be appointed
to a school with a residence if possible, or where a residence is
procurable, in a cool climate. I would like an appointment in the
Lithgow or Bathurst district if practicable." Reply from Chief Inspector
goes on to write "As he has been teaching Aborigines for so long and in
view of the other circumstances above stated, I recommend that he be
removed to a Public School at a place where there is a residence
available."

14/12/1899 -- Reporting damage to building during recent storm -- "heavy
winds have caused this School to swerve, and the corner post (pine) has
split. I would advise that it be braced up and a new post put in as soon
as possible because I believe wind as heavy as we have had during last
week would probably blow it over unless it is stayed." Note from Chief
Inspector -- This building is the property of the Aborigines Protection
Board. Specification for repairs are attached. I recommend that the
damage be reported to the Aborigines Protection Board.

9/2/1901 -- Application for information concerning the tank at this
School repairs necessary -- "I have the honor to inform you that some
time during the late vacation, the tank at this school was blown about
by the wind and damaged so as to render it useless at present for
holding water."

7/10/1912 -- To Inspector of Schools Young -- Close the School for the

25/5/1920 -- Extract from Sanitary report -- Water Supply (Drinking) --
One common drinking Vessel in use. Structural condition -- No urinal for
the boys. Playground -- No Weather Shed. School Building -- No Cloak
Room. Desks are all multiple and no backs to seats. No ventilation.

9/9/1929 -- The floor is broken in several places, accidents are likely
to occur, unless repairs are effected. It is recommended that the
Aborigines Protection Board be notified of the condition and that it be
required to have some remedied.

16/5/1934 -- Repairs and improvements to school

The school room at Erambie I consider unsuitable at present. The
ventilation and lighting are quite unsatisfactory, and the ventilation
should be improved as soon as possible. The effective enrolment is 42 at
present and likely to increase. The windows are too small and too few. I
consider the whole building needs re-modeling or a new building erected.
I understand the building belongs to the AP Board.

**Curriculum and Resources**

12/12/1905 -- I beg to report that according to instructions I attended
at the Cowra Superior Public School on Monday 11^th^ inst, to observe
the working of the "New Syllabus" I have gained a good insight into the
working of the Syllabus and could apply it in any school to which I may
be appointed." Baylour Morgan Teacher.

Baylour Morgan 8/11/1905 -- "I beg to state that on 21^st^ March 1904 I
was appointed to this school for Aborigines of whom there are twelve
(12) enrolled. On 1^st^ May last I gained a 3A classification be
examination -- being previously unclassified. Therefore I expect to be
removed from this school at an early date. The subjects taught to
Aboriginal children are Reading, Writing and Arithmetic only. Thus it
will be seen that I am unacquainted with the new teaching as embodied in
the "New Syllabus". In order to gain an insight into the working of the
"New Syllabus", I beg permission to attend at the Public School, Cowra
for such time as the Department sees fit to allow. I reside in Cowra and
would suggest one different day in each week be granted me, thus
enabling me to observe the complete course of instruction."

19/12/1932 -- Sewing lessons to Aboriginal Children -- Sir, For many
years I have been giving instruction to Aboriginal children without
receiving any remuneration from the Department. To give these children a
thorough training in and Fancy Sewing which includes other branches of
needlework, such as Beads, Rafia, etc, takes approximately four hours
per week. On average I spend about five hours of my time in the school
each week. At present there are twelve girls and four boys giving
instruction. I would be grateful if some slight remuneration would be
granted to me for these services, commencing in the year 1933."

**Other comments**

APB - 23/11/1938 -- Aborigines School Erambie -- Alteration of School
hours -- "Arrangements have recently been made by the Board with the
Director General of Public Health for the visitation to those Stations
under its control where same is possible of the local Baby Health Nurse.
The Nurse examines the children on the Station and advises mothers as to
their welfare. One of the Stations thus affected is Cowra and the
Manager -- Teacher reports that the only room available on the Station
in which the examinations can be carried out is the school, and it is
necessary for him to cease teaching between the hours of 10 am and noon
on Wednesday so that the school can be made available. As it is
certainly not the desire of the Board to interfere with the education of
the children and pending other arrangements being made in the direction
of erecting a room suitable for the use of the Nurse, it is requested
that approval be given for the extension of the school hours for half an
hour on each of the 4 days of the week.
